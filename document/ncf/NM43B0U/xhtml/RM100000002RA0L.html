COIL SPRING AND SHOCK ABSORBER

CONSTRUCTION

(a) A dual-rate spring is used for the coil spring. As a result, ideal ride comfort regardless of load conditions is achieved. Polyurethane tubes are placed above and below the spring and a super single coating*1 or dual coating*2 is used, ensuring anti-corrosive characteristics.

HINT:

*1: Except models with 2.5" TRD Lift Kit

*2: Models with 2.5" TRD Lift Kit

(b) A urethane rear bumper stopper and hydraulic hollow spring sub-assembly* are used to reduce the impact generated when the suspension reaches full travel, and to improve riding comfort.

HINT:

*: Models with TRD Pro package option

(c) A low-pressure gas (N2)-filled type shock absorber is used to provide stable damping force characteristics, and to achieve a higher dimension of both maneuverability/stability and riding comfort.

(1) A triple lip oil seal*1 or double lip oil seal*2 is used to ensure superior oil leak performance.

(2) A long type dust cover is used to ensure superior anti-dust performance.

(3) The damping force can be adjusted in 3 stages using the Quick Switch 3 (QS3) built into the shock absorber.*3

HINT:

*1: Except models with TRD Pro package option or TrailHunter grade package option

*2: Models with TRD Pro package option or TrailHunter grade package option

*3: Models with TRD Pro package option

(d) The rear shock absorber is installed outside of the frame, ensuring superior driving stability and a comfortable ride.

Except Models with TRD Off-Road, TRD Pro Package Option or TrailHunter Grade Package Option
