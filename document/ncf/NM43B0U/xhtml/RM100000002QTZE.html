GENERAL

OUTLINE

(a) The hybrid system of this hybrid electrical vehicle employs the Parallel Hybrid.

(b) A Parallel Hybrid is used to achieve highly responsive performance using motor assist.

(c) Hybrid electrical vehicles use a combination of 2 kinds of power sources, such as an engine and electric motor, so as to take advantage of the benefits provided by each power source while compensating for each other's shortcomings. As a result, efficient operation is achieved.

(d) Hybrid electrical vehicles do not need their batteries to be charged externally unlike conventional electric-only vehicles. Therefore, special infrastructure is not required to use hybrid vehicles.

(e) The hybrid system is a flexible system that uses a high-efficiency power unit and an electric motors.

(f) Hybrid electrical vehicles have high-voltage electrical circuits. Hybrid electrical vehicles have been developed with consideration given to the protection of drivers and technicians against electrocution.

MAIN FEATURES

(a) Hybrid electrical vehicles have the following features:

Features

Outline

Idle Stop (Reduction of Energy Loss)

Idling of the engine is automatically stopped (idle stop) to reduce energy loss.

EV Drive (Efficient Drive Control)

This allows the vehicle to be driven using only the electric motor when engine efficiency is low. In addition, electricity is generated when engine efficiency is high. Control is performed to maximize the total efficiency of the vehicle.

Motor Assist

An electric motor supplements the engine power when accelerating.

Regenerative Braking (Energy Regeneration)

During deceleration and while depressing the brake pedal, part of the energy that was lost as heat is collected as electrical energy to be reused, for example as motor power.

(2) The Parallel Hybrid makes it possible to directly transmit the engine motive force from the high-output T24A-FTS engine and the MG motive force from the MG in the hybrid module assembly, when combined with the automatic transmission, it demonstrates excellent acceleration capability.

(3) Hybrid electrical vehicles have 2 batteries that are used for different purposes. One is the HV supply battery assembly (nominal voltage of DC 288 V) that stores electrical power to drive the vehicle, and the other is the auxiliary battery (nominal voltage of DC 12 V) that supplies electrical power to the electrical components.

(4) Furthermore, hybrid electrical vehicles use a variable-voltage system consisting of a high-output HV supply battery assembly with a nominal voltage of DC 288 V and an inverter that converts direct current and alternating current

(5) Since hybrid electrical vehicles are not equipped with a conventional alternator, the high-voltage from the HV supply battery assembly is stepped down to approximately DC 14 V using a DC/DC converter in order to charge the auxiliary battery. Also, the HV supply battery assembly regularly charges and discharges within the constant State of Charge (SOC) range while the vehicle is running, so recharging from external power sources is not necessary.

(6) The engine can be started using the MG or the starter assembly, ensuring startability in cold regions. The engine can be started using the starter assembly at regular intervals to improve reliability.

PRECAUTION

(a) Hybrid Electrical Vehicles High-voltage Safety Measures

(1) High-voltage safety is comprised of 2 points: insulation of high-voltage circuits and cut-off of high-voltage circuits. The hybrid system also detects whether or not a decrease in insulation resistance has occurred between the high-voltage system and body ground.

(b) Insulation of High-voltage Circuits

(1) High-voltage circuits are used between the HV supply battery assembly, hybrid motor control inverter assembly, hybrid module assembly and compressor with motor assembly. Each of these items is connected by a power cable and electrically insulated using cases and covers.

(2) Cables are also shielded using a mesh conductor built into the electrical insulation of the wires. The shielding is grounded to the chassis of the vehicle and the main purpose is to prevent electromagnetic interference.

(c) Cut-off of High-voltage Circuits

(1) When any of the conditions below occurs, the System Main Relays (SMRs) are automatically shut off by the hybrid vehicle control ECU assembly.

  • Ignition switch is turned off.
  • Any airbag is deployed.
  • Upper inverter cover sub-assembly is removed (interlock circuit is open).
  • Connector of power cable (floor under wire on the hybrid motor control inverter assembly side) is disconnected (interlock circuit is open).
  • Service plug grip handle is raised partway (interlock circuit is open)*.
  • Specified malfunction occurs.

    HINT:

    *: The service plug grip should never be removed when the vehicle is in the on (READY) state.

(2) The service plug grip is used to cut off the high-voltage circuit manually for when service is to be performed on the vehicle.

CAUTION:

Regarding discharge of the capacitor in the hybrid motor control inverter assembly: a charge remains in the high-voltage capacitor in the hybrid motor control inverter assembly after the high voltage circuits are shut down. When servicing a hybrid electrical vehicles, after the service plug grip is removed, wait at least 10 minutes to allow the capacitor in the inverter to discharge before beginning work.
