  • Outputs the illumination signals to the AIRBAG ON/OFF indicator lights.

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) Summary

(1) In the occupant classification system, the airbag ECU calculates the weight of the occupant based on signals from the occupant classification sensors.

(2) The airbag ECU contains criteria value "A" to judge whether the seat is occupied by a child or is unoccupied in accordance with the signals from the 2 occupant classification sensors and front passenger seat belt buckle switch, and criteria value "B" to judge whether the occupant is an adult or child (with child seat).

(3) This system recognizes the occupant as a child or child seat if it detects a weight of less than criteria value "B", and disables the SRS front passenger airbag.

(b) Unoccupied Judgment

(1) The airbag ECU makes an unoccupied judgment when the judgment value is lower than criteria value "A" and the front passenger seat belt buckle switch is off.

(2) If the ignition switch is turned to ON in this state, the system performs an initial check, and illuminates the AIRBAG OFF indicator light. Then, the system prohibits the deployment of the SRS front passenger airbag, SRS front passenger knee airbag and SRS front passenger side airbag.

(c) Child Seat or Child Judgment

(1) If the judgment value is lower than criteria value "B" and the front passenger seat belt buckle switch is on, the airbag ECU judges that a child seat is installed.

(2) If the judgment value is higher than criteria value "A", but lower than criteria value "B", and the front passenger seat belt buckle switch is off, the airbag ECU judges that the seat is being occupied by a child.

(3) When the ignition switch is turned to ON under these conditions, the system performs an initial check and illuminates the AIRBAG OFF indicator light to indicate that the SRS front passenger airbag, SRS front passenger knee airbag and SRS front passenger side airbag have been deactivated.

(d) Adult Judgment

(1) When the judgment value is higher than criteria value "B", the airbag ECU judges that the seat is occupied by an adult.

(2) If the ignition switch is turned to ON in this state, the system performs an initial check and illuminates the AIRBAG ON indicator light, indicating that the SRS front passenger airbag, SRS front passenger knee airbag, SRS front passenger side airbag and front passenger seat belt pretensioner are active.

DIAGNOSIS

(a) If the airbag ECU detects a malfunction in the occupant classification system, the airbag ECU stores the malfunction data in memory. If the airbag ECU detects a malfunction in the airbag ECU, the airbag ECU illuminates the SRS warning light and AIRBAG OFF indicator light.

(b) The airbag ECU outputs Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) to the Global TechStream (GTS).

(c) The DTCs can be read by connecting the Global TechStream (GTS) to the DLC3. For details, refer to the Repair Manual.
