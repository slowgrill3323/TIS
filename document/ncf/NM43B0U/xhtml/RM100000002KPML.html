OPERATING CONDITION

(a) Drive Force Suppression Control

(1) When all of the following conditions are met, drive force suppression control is performed.

Condition

Forward or Rearward Stationary Object
  • The ignition switch is on (IG).
  • The parking support brake system is on.
  • An stationary object is in the traveling direction of the driver's own vehicle.
  • The vehicle speed is 15 km/h (9 mph) or less.
  • The driver can avoid the object by braking operations.

Vehicle Approaching from Rear*1
  • The ignition switch is on (IG).

  • The shift position is R.

  • The vehicle speed is 15 km/h (9 mph) or less.

NOTICE:

When brake control operates, the parking support brake system automatically turns off. The system remains off until it is turned on by an operation on the multi-display screen, the vehicle reverses approximately 300 mm (11.8 in.) or more*1 or approximately the entire length of the vehicle*2, the vehicle moves forward approximately the entire length of the vehicle*2, or the ignition switch is turned off and back on (IG).

  • *1: A vehicle approaching from the rear is detected.

    *2: A stationary object is detected.

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) Parking Support Brake (Stationary Objects) Detection Range

(1) The ultrasonic sensor detects stationary objects that come within approximately 4 m (13 ft.). In addition, the detection distance may differ depending on the shape of the stationary object.

(2) When the vehicle is moving forward, the parking support brake (static objects) operates when all the front ultrasonic sensors detect a static object in the path of the vehicle. When the vehicle is moving forward, the parking support brake (static objects) does not operate unless two or more front ultrasonic sensors detect a static object at the same time.

(3) When the vehicle is moving rearward, the parking support brake (static objects) operates when all the rear ultrasonic sensors detect a static object in the path of the vehicle. When the vehicle is moving rearward, the parking support brake (static objects) does not operate unless two or more rear ultrasonic sensors detect a static object at the same time.

(b) Parking Support Brake System Control

(1) The parking support brake system determines the state of the vehicle and its surroundings based on signals that the clearance warning ECU assembly receives from sensors, switches and ECUs installed in various locations on the vehicle, and sends the necessary control signals to ECUs to control the vehicle.

(2) When the system operates, a warning message is displayed in the multi-information display of the combination meter assembly, multi-display or headup display*, and the meter buzzer sounds to warn the driver.

  • *: Models with headup display

(3) The parking support brake system detects stationary objects using sensors, and when it determines that there is a risk of collision, suppress the drive force.

Drive Force Suppression Control

(4) When the system determines that there is a high possibility of a collision even after the above operations, brake control is performed in addition to drive force suppression control.

FUNCTION

(a) Parking Support Brake System Operation

(1) The parking support brake system can be changed to ON/OFF using the "Driving assist" menu on the multi-display.

(2) The driving assist information indicator is located in the combination meter assembly and enables the driver to check whether the parking support brake system is ON/OFF. When the system is off, a message is displayed on the multi-information display and the driving assist information indicator illuminates.

FAIL-SAFE

(a) When the clearance warning ECU assembly detects a malfunction in the system, the system enters fail-safe mode. For details, refer to the Repair Manual.

DIAGNOSIS

(a) In order to make system inspections easier to perform, a diagnosis function is used in consideration of serviceability. The DTCs of malfunctioning parts can be read by connecting the Global TechStream (GTS). Refer to the Repair Manual for details.
