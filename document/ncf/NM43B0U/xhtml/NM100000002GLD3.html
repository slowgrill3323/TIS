Title: PARK ASSIST / MONITORING: INTUITIVE PARKING ASSIST SYSTEM: GENERAL; 2024 - 2026 MY Tacoma Tacoma HV [12/2023 -        ]

GENERAL

OUTLINE

(a) The intuitive parking assist system uses ultrasonic sensors to detect static objects in front of, at the corners or to the rear of the vehicle.

(b) The intuitive parking assist system informs the driver of the approximate distance between the ultrasonic sensors and any static objects as well as their positions by displaying them on the multi-information display in the combination meter assembly or radio and display receiver assembly and by sounding the meter buzzer.

(c) A mute function for the meter buzzer is provided. When the meter buzzer sounds due to the detection of an obstacle, the buzzer can be temporarily muted.

PRECAUTION

(a) The intuitive parking assist is supplemental equipment that notifies the driver by buzzer and display and distance of stationary objects during low-speed driving. [1]

(b) Precautions to observe during use [2]

(1) There are some restrictions to the detection range and operation speed of the sensor. When driving the vehicle in forward or reverse, be sure to confirm that the area around the vehicle is safe (especially on the sides of the vehicle), use the brakes to control the vehicle speed and drive slowly.

(2) If an abnormality is displayed, first confirm the status of the sensor. If an abnormality is displayed even though there is no ice, snow or dirt, the sensor may be operating abnormally. Have it inspected by your Toyota dealer.

(3) Do not install aftermarket electrical components (backlit license plates, fog lights, fender poles, radio antennas, etc.) in the vicinity of the sensor.

(4) Do not modify, disassemble, or paint the sensor.

(5) Always keep the sensor clean, but be careful not to scratch or otherwise damage it.

(6) Contact your Toyota dealer when removal and installation or replacement of the front or rear bumper or grille is necessary.

(7) Do not install a license plate cover.

(8) Keep the tires inflated to the appropriate pressure.

(c) In the following situations, a sensor abnormality may prevent the equipment from operating properly. Have the vehicle inspected by your Toyota dealer. [3]

(1) The detection display is illuminated or blinks, or the buzzer sounds when no stationary object is detected.

(2) The area around the sensor is struck by an object, hit or otherwise subject to a strong impact.

(3) The bumper is struck.

(d) Precautions to observe when washing the vehicle [4]

(1) When washing the vehicle in a high pressure car washer, do not allow water to make direct contact with the sensor area. The sensor may stop operating normally if subject to impact from high pressure water.

(2) When washing the vehicle in a washer that uses steam, do not allow the steam to come too close to the sensor area. Steam may cause the sensor to stop operating normally.

(e) In the following situations, turn off the parking assist sensor system.

(1) The system may not be able to detect stationary objects, or may not operate correctly.

(2) When the suspension installed to the vehicle is not the Toyota genuine one.

(3) When the instructions in items [1] through [4] above cannot be followed.

(f) Precautions during use

(1) Do not allow the speed of the vehicle to exceed 10 km/h (6.2 mph).

(2) The detection range of the sensor is limited to the vehicle corners and around the front/rear bumpers.

(3) The shape and condition of the stationary object may reduce the detection range or prevent detection.

(4) The sensor/camera may be unable to detect a stationary object if it is too close to it.

(5) After a stationary object is detected, it may take a short amount of time for the indicator to operate and the buzzer to sound. The stationary object may approach the non-detection range before the indicator operates and the buzzer sounds, even when driving at low speeds.

(6) When playing audio or using the air conditioning, sounds such as music or the fan noise may make it difficult to hear the buzzer sound.

(7) Sounds such as buzzers from other systems may make it difficult to hear sounds from this system.

(8) The buzzer may not sound when the meter is malfunctioning.

(g) The sensor may not be able to detect stationary objects in the following situations. Be sure to drive carefully.

(1) The sensor/camera is covered by ice, snow or dirt (returns to normal when the sensor is cleaned).

(2) The sensor/camera is frozen (returns to normal when not frozen).

(3) The sensor/camera is covered by an object such as a hand.

(4) In blazing heat or freezing cold.

(5) The vehicle is being driven on undulating roads, sloped roads, unpaved roads, in tall grass or similar conditions.

(6) The vehicle is close to a source of ultrasonic waves, such as a vehicle horn or sensor system, motorcycle engine, large vehicle air brakes or intuitive parking assist equipment on another vehicle.

(7) When heavy rain or water is sprayed on the sensor.

(8) Objects not perpendicular to the road surface, objects not at right angles to the vehicle's direction of travel, objects with irregular surface textures, or objects with an undulating or wavy shape.

(9) In strong wind.

(10) In inclement weather such as fog, snow, or a sandstorm.

(11) When there is an undetectable object between the vehicle and an object that the system would otherwise react to.

(12) When a detectable object such as a car, motorcycle, bicycle, pedestrian, etc. cuts in from the side of the vehicle or appears suddenly.

(13) When accessories such as a towing hook, bumper protector, bumper trim, bicycle carrier, snow removal device (snowplow), etc. are installed in the vicinity of the sensor.

(14) When there are extreme changes to vehicle height (nose-up, nose-down)

(15) The vehicle handling is unstable due to an accident or malfunction.

(16) Tire chains, an emergency spare tire, or a tire repaired with an emergency puncture repair kit are being used.

(h) The sensor may not be able to detect the following objects. Be sure to drive carefully.

(1) Thin objects such as wire, fences and rope.

(2) Objects that absorb sound waves such as strings or snow.

(3) Objects with sharp corners.

(4) Short or low to the ground objects.

(5) Tall objects where the top sticks out.

(6) A pedestrian is wearing clothes that do not reflect ultrasonic waves well (such as a puffy or frilled skirt)).

(i) The system may not operate correctly in the following situations:

(1) The vehicle is driving on narrow roads.

(2) The vehicle is driving towards banners or curtains, flags, hanging branches, or boom barrier gates (such as railroad cross bars, toll booth arms, parking lot gates, etc.)

(3) The road surface has wheel ruts or potholes.

(4) The vehicle is driving over a metal grating on a drainage channel.

(5) The vehicle is driving on steep uphill or downhill slopes.

(6) The sensor is covered by water on a flooded road.

(7) Ice, snow, or mud is adhering to the sensor.

(8) When heavy rain or water is sprayed on the sensor.

(9) In inclement weather such as fog, snow, or a sandstorm.

(10) In strong wind.

(11) The vehicle is in proximity to a source of ultrasonic waves, such as a vehicle horn, motorcycle engine, the air brakes of a large vehicle or the ultrasonic sensor system of another vehicle.

(12) When there are extreme changes to vehicle height (nose-up, nose-down).

(13) The aim of the ultrasonic sensor has become misaligned due to a collision or other impact.

(14) The vehicle is driving toward a high curb or a right-angle curb.

(15) The vehicle is driving near a pillar (H-section steel) in a parking structure or construction site.

(16) The vehicle handling is unstable due to an accident or malfunction.

(17) The vehicle is driving on rough, hilly, gravelly or grassy roads.

(18) Tire chains, an emergency spare tire, or a tire repaired with an emergency puncture repair kit are being used.

(19) When the areas around the vehicle is dark or backlit.

(20) Wireless antenna or fog light near the vehicle.

(21) When a parked vehicle is too close to the sensor/camera.

(22) The vehicle is being driven toward a high curb or perpendicular curb.

(23) A stationary object is in the area directly below a bumper/camera.

(24) No obstacle is detected by a camera.

(25) A suspension other than a Toyota genuine suspension is installed.
