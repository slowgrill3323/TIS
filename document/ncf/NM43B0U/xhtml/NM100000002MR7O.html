SHIFT LOCK MECHANISM

FUNCTION OF MAIN COMPONENTS

Component

Function

Shift Lock Solenoid Assembly

Shift Lock Solenoid

Locks the shift lever in P.

P Detection Switch

Detects the shift lever in P.

Shift Lock Control ECU

Controls the shift lock solenoid based on signals from each switch.

Stop Light Switch Assembly

Detects when the brake pedal is depressed.

Power Switch (Push Start Switch)

Detects the switch operation condition.

Certification ECU (Smart Key ECU Assembly)

Sends the engine switch signal to the shift lock control ECU sub-assembly.

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) The shift lock control ECU uses the P detection switch to detect the shift lever position, and receives input signals from the stop lamp switch assembly and ignition switch. Upon receiving these signals, the shift lock control ECU turns on the shift lock solenoid in order to release the shift lock.

FUNCTION

(a) Shift Lock Mechanism

(1) The shift lock mechanism prevents the shift lever from being moved to any position other than P, unless the ignition switch is ON, and the brake pedal is depressed. This mechanism helps to prevent unintentional acceleration.

(2) A shift lock release button, which manually overrides the shift lock mechanism, is provided.
