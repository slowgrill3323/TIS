PISTON

CONSTRUCTION

(a) The pistons are made of aluminum alloy to allow them to be compact and lightweight.

(b) By using spherical shaped piston tops, air disturbance is minimized and a high speed combustion is realized. Accordingly, knocking is minimized.

(c) The groove for the No. 1 compression ring is a Ni-resist cast iron insert suitable for high power output.

(d) A Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD) coating have been applied to the surface of the No. 1 compression ring, in order to reduce friction losses and improve wear resistance.

(e) A Diamond Like Carbon (DLC) coating have been applied to the surface of the oil ring side rail, in order to reduce friction losses and improve wear resistance.

(f) To prevent increased oil consumption at high engine speeds, an asymmetric barrel design has been adopted for the outer circumference of the side rails of the oil ring. As well as realizing a drastic reduction in oil consumption, friction has been reduced due to optimization of the oil ring tension.

(g) An oil ring expander which utilizes punched grooves to prevent deposits from accumulating is used. Oil flow between the expander and side rails is enhanced, realizing a structure that prevents deposits from accumulating.

(h) By applying a streak finish to the piston skirt together with smoothing by the application of a resin coating, bore friction resistance has been reduced.
