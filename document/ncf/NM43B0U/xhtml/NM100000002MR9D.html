FOUR WHEEL DRIVE CONTROL

FUNCTION OF MAIN COMPONENTS

Component

Function

Transfer Shift Actuator Assembly

High-low Shift Motor

Switches the transfer gear ratio between high and low in accordance with signals from the 4 wheel drive control ECU.

High-low Limit Switch

Detects the position of the high-low shift motor and transmits a signal to the 4 wheel drive control ECU.

Center Differential Lock Shift Motor

Switches the center differential between free and lock in accordance with signals from the 4 wheel drive control ECU.

Center Differential Lock Limit Switch

Detects the position of the center differential lock shift motor and transmits a signal to the 4 wheel drive control ECU.

Center Differential Lock Position Switch

Detects the lock position of the center differential and transmits a signal to the 4 wheel drive control ECU and so on.

Integration Control and Panel Assembly

Front-wheel Drive Control Switch
  • Switches the drive mode and transmits a signal to the 4 wheel drive control ECU.
  • Switches the center differential between free and lock and transmits a signal to the 4 wheel drive control ECU.

4 Wheel Drive Control ECU

Controls each shift motor in response to a signal from each switch and ECU.

Hybrid Vehicle Control ECU Assembly

Transmits a shift position signal to the 4 wheel drive control ECU via CAN.

Brake Actuator Assembly

No. 2 Skid Control ECU

Transmits a vehicle speed signal to the 4 wheel drive control ECU via CAN.

Combination Meter Assembly

Low Speed Four-wheel Drive Indicator
  • Blinks while switching the transfer gear ratio between high and low, and turns on when in low.
  • Blinks when the switching conditions are not met while switching the transfer gear ratio between high and low.

Center Differential Lock Indicator
  • Blinks while switching the center differential between free and lock, and turns on when in lock.
  • Blinks when the switching conditions are not met while switching the center differential between free and lock.

VSC OFF Indicator

Turns on in accordance with the drive mode when VSC function is not operating.

Buzzer

Sounds when the switching conditions are not met while switching the drive mode.

(a) The drive mode switching pattern of the 4WD system is as shown in the illustration below:

(b) When the vehicle speed is 100km/h (62mph) or less, free-lock switching of the center differential lock (switching patterns A or B) is available while the vehicle is running.

(c) The transfer gear ratio can be switched between high and low (switching patterns C or D) only when the vehicle is stationary and the shift lever is in N.

(d) VSC function in the brake control system may be stopped, depending on the drive mode. For details, see the Brake Control/Dynamic Control Systems section.

DIAGNOSIS

(a) When the 4 wheel drive control ECU detects a malfunction, the 4 wheel drive control ECU records the malfunction and memorizes the information related to the fault.

(b) The 4 wheel drive control ECU will also store the Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) of the malfunctions. The DTCs stored in the 4 wheel drive control ECU are output to the GTS via the DLC3.

(c) For details, refer to the Repair Manual.
