HEATED STEERING WHEEL CONTROL

FUNCTION OF MAIN COMPONENTS

Component

Function

Steering Wheel Assembly

Thermostat

Stops current flow in the heater element when the steering wheel assembly surface temperature exceeds a threshold.

Heater Element

Increases the steering wheel assembly surface temperature using power supplied by the multiplex network steering ECU.

Thermistor

Sends a steering wheel assembly surface temperature signal to the multiplex network steering ECU.

Multiplex Network Steering ECU

Performs heated steering wheel control according to signals from the air conditioning amplifier assembly and thermistor.

Air Conditioning Control Assembly

Steering Heater Switch

  • Indicator Light
  • Sends a steering heater switch on signal to the air conditioning amplifier assembly.
  • Illuminates when the heated steering wheel system is on.

Air Conditioning Amplifier Assembly

Outputs steering heater control request signals to the multiplex network steering ECU.

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) When the steering heater switch (air conditioning control assembly) is turned on with the ignition switch is on (IG), current flows to the heater element inside the steering wheel assembly, and the heater element is heated. As a result, the heating area of the steering wheel assembly is heated. In addition, the indicator light in the steering heater switch (air conditioning control assembly) turns on to notify the driver that the heated steering wheel system is in operation.

(b) The heated steering wheel system detects the heater wire temperature from the thermistor* inside the steering wheel assembly, controls the amount of current to the heater element, andmaintains the heater portion at 44°C for the steering wheel assembly.

HINT:

*: An element that uses a semiconductor whose electrical resistance changes significantly in response to slight changes in temperature (the resistance is high at normal temperatures, but it decreases as the temperature increases).

(c) When the heater wire temperature increases to approximately 55°C or higher, the thermostat* stops supplying electric current to the heater element. When the heater wire temperature increasesto approximately 30°C, the thermostat starts supplying electric current again.

HINT:

*: This is a temperature adjustment device which automatically controls a heat source by using a switch based on bimetal and shape-memory alloy.

FAIL-SAFE

(a) When a system malfunction occurs, the multiplex network steering ECU stops the supply of electric current to the heater element to ensure safety in accordance with the fail-safe function. For details, refer to the Repair Manual.
