AIRBAG ECU ASSEMBLY

CONSTRUCTION

(a) The airbag ECU consists of judgment circuits, a deceleration sensor, an ignition control circuit, a safing sensor, a backup power circuit, a diagnostic circuit and a memory circuit.

Item

Outline
*: Double cab models

Judgment Circuit

Judgment circuits are built into the airbag ECU to judge the severity of a collision using the acceleration/deceleration signals from the front airbag sensors, side airbag pressure sensors, floor side airbag sensors and No. 2 side airbag sensors*.

Deceleration Sensor

A deceleration sensor is built into the airbag ECU. This sensor distorts in the event of a collision. The amount of distortion is based on the deceleration rate of the vehicle during a frontal collision and is converted into an electric signal.

Ignition Control Circuit

The ignition control circuit performs calculations based on the signal output from the judgment circuit of the airbag ECU, front airbag sensors, side airbag pressure sensors, floor side airbag sensors and No. 2 side airbag sensors*. If the calculated values are greater than the specified values, the SRS airbags are deployed.

Safing Sensor

During a frontal and side collision, this sensor turns on and outputs an on signal to the airbag ECU if the deceleration rate of the safing sensor is greater than a specified value.

Backup Power Circuit

The backup power circuit consists of a power supply capacitor and a DC-DC converter. When the power supply to the airbag ECU is disrupted during a collision, the power supply capacitor discharges and supplies electric power to the system. The DC-DC converter operates as a boosting transformer when the battery voltage falls below a predetermined level.

Diagnostic Circuit

The diagnostic circuit constantly monitors the system for malfunctions. When a malfunction is detected, it turns on the SRS warning indicator in the combination meter assembly to inform the driver.

Memory Circuit

When a malfunction is detected by the diagnostic circuit, a DTC is stored in memory.
