Airbag ECU (Airbag ECU Assembly)
  • In the event of a frontal collision, the airbag ECU receives signals from either of the front airbag sensors. The airbag ECU receives the signal via its judgment circuit and also receives another signal from the safing sensor built into the airbag ECU. After receiving a signal from at least one of the front airbag sensors and the safing sensor, the airbag ECU determines whether the SRS driver airbag, SRS front passenger airbag, SRS driver knee airbag, SRS front passenger knee airbag and front and rear seat belt pretensioners*1 should be deployed. The airbag ECU also diagnoses system malfunctions.
  • In the event of a side collision, the airbag ECU receives signals from the side airbag pressure sensor, No. 2 side airbag sensor*1 and floor side airbag sensor. The airbag ECU receives the signal via its judgment circuit and also receives another signal from the safing sensor built into the airbag ECU. After receiving a signal from at least one of the side sensors and the safing sensor, the airbag ECU determines whether the SRS front side airbags, SRS curtain shield airbags, front seat belt pretensioners and rear seat belt pretensioners*1 should be deployed. The airbag ECU also diagnoses system malfunctions.

DCM (Telephone Transceiver)*2

OPERATING CONDITION

(a) Frontal Collision

(1) Deceleration sensors used for the SRS airbag system are installed on various parts on the vehicle and calculate the deceleration (or acceleration) rate of each part during a collision.

(2) Depending on the situation, the airbag ECU sends a deployment signal to each SRS airbag based on the information from each sensor.

(3) Frontal collision signals are produced based on the information from the airbag ECU and front airbag sensors. Frontal collision signals are used to deploy the SRS airbags and activate the front seat belt pretensioners and rear seat belt pretensioners* as indicated in the table below.

HINT:

*: Double cab models

(4) If the severity of the collision is particularly high, the SRS curtain shield airbags LH and SRS curtain shield airbags RH are deployed.

(b) Side Collision (Front)

(1) The SRS front side airbag and SRS curtain shield airbag on the front side of the collision is deployed and the front seat belt pretensioner and rear seat belt pretensioner* on the front side of the collision are activated based on signals from the side airbag pressure sensor, floor side airbag sensor and airbag ECU.

HINT:

*: Double cab models

(2) If the severity of the collision is particularly high, the SRS curtain shield airbag assembly on the side opposite the collision is deployed and the front seat belt pretensioner and rear seat belt pretensioner* on the side opposite the collision are activated.

HINT:

  • *: Double cab models
  • During a front side collision, airbags are not deployed based on only signals from the floor side airbag sensor (based on only the safing sensor signal of the floor side airbag sensor and main electronic sensor signal of the floor side airbag sensor).

(c) Side Collision (Rear)

(1) The SRS curtain shield airbag on the side of the collision is deployed and the rear seat belt pretensioner* on the side of the collision is activated based on signals from the No. 2 side airbag sensor*, floor side airbag sensor and airbag ECU.

HINT:

*: Double cab models

(2) If the severity of the collision is particularly high, the SRS curtain shield airbag on the side opposite the collision is deployed and the rear seat belt pretensioner* on the side opposite the collision is activated.

HINT:

*: Double cab models

(d) Roll Over

(1) The SRS curtain shield airbag assemblies LH and RH are deployed and the front and rear seat belt pretensioners* are activated based on signals that show the vehicle's roll angle and rotation speed.

HINT:

*: Double cab models

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) Frontal Collision

(1) SRS airbags which deploy in the event of a frontal collision: the SRS driver airbag, SRS front passenger airbag, SRS driver knee airbag, SRS passenger knee airbag, SRS curtain shield airbag. These SRS airbags deploy simultaneously. The SRS driver airbag and SRS front passenger airbag use dual-stage control. In addition, the seat belt pretensioners contained in the front seat outer belt assembly LH, front seat outer belt assembly RH, rear seat outer belt assembly LH*, rear seat outer belt assembly RH* are also activated.

(b) Side Collision (Front)

(1) SRS airbags which deploy in the event of a front side collision: the SRS front side airbag and SRS curtain shield airbag on the side of the collision. These SRS airbags deploy simultaneously. Additionally, at the same time, the front seat belt pretensioners and rear seat belt pretensioners* on the front side of the collision are also activated.

HINT:

*: Double cab models

(2) For a front side collision, if the side airbag pressure sensor and floor side airbag sensor detect an impact, it informs the airbag ECU. The airbag ECU causes the SRS front side airbag and SRS curtain shield airbag on the front side of the collision to deploy simultaneously, and activates the front seat belt pretensioner and rear seat belt pretensioner* on the front side of the collision.

HINT:

*: Double cab models

(3) If the severity of the collision is particularly high, the SRS curtain shield airbag on the side opposite the collision is deployed and the front seat belt pretensioner and rear seat belt pretensioner* on the side opposite the collision are activated.

HINT:

*: Double cab models

(c) Side Collision (Rear)

(1) The SRS curtain shield airbag on the side of the collision in the event of a rear side collision. Additionally, at the same time, the rear seat belt pretensioner on the rear side of the collision is also activated.

(2) For a rear side collision, if the No. 2 side airbag sensor and floor side airbag sensor detects an impact, it informs the airbag ECU and the airbag ECU causes the SRS curtain shield airbag on the side of the collision to deploy simultaneously, and activates the rear seat belt pretensioner on the rear side of the collision.

(3) If the severity of the collision is particularly high, the SRS curtain shield airbag on the side opposite the collision is deployed and the rear seat belt pretensioner on the side opposite the collision is activated.

(d) Roll Over

(1) If the vehicle is tilted and rolls over, the airbag ECU activates the SRS curtain shield airbag assemblies LH and RH and seat belt pretensioners for the driver, front passenger and rear passengers* based on signals that show the vehicle's roll angle and rotation speed.

HINT:

*: Double cab models

(2) If the vehicle rolls over in a side collision, the airbag ECU causes the SRS side airbag and SRS curtain shield airbag assembly on the side of the collision to deploy simultaneously, and activates the front and rear seat belt pretensioners* on the side of the collision based on signals from the side airbag pressure sensor, No. 2 side airbag sensor assembly* and floor side airbag sensor. Meanwhile, the airbag ECU activates the SRS curtain shield airbag assembly on the side opposite the collision and the front and rear seat belt pretensioners* on the side opposite the collision, based on lateral deceleration and rotation speed signals.

HINT:

*: Double cab models

(e) Primary Check Illumination Control

(1) After the ignition switch is turned to ON, the airbag ECU illuminates the SRS warning indicator in the combination meter assembly for approximately 6 seconds and conducts the primary check. During the primary check, the ignition of SRS airbags is prohibited and a diagnosis of the airbag ECU is performed. If a malfunction is detected during the primary check, the SRS warning indicator will remain on even after 6 seconds have elapsed.

DIAGNOSIS

(a) If the airbag ECU detects a malfunction in the SRS airbag system, the airbag ECU stores the malfunction data in memory in addition to illuminating the SRS warning indicator.

(b) The airbag ECU outputs malfunction data and Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) to the Global TechStream (GTS).

(c) The DTCs can be read by connecting the Global TechStream (GTS) to the DLC3. For details, refer to the Repair Manual.

(d) If the SRS airbags deploy, the airbag ECU will turn on the SRS warning indicator. However, unlike an ordinary malfunction, a DTCs will not be stored. The SRS warning indicator cannot be manually turned off. It is necessary to replace the airbag ECU with a new one.
