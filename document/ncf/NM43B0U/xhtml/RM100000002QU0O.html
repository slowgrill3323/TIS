SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) The cargo lights and bed lights are controlled as follows:

Control

Condition

Turn On

The cargo lights and bed lights illuminate when the shift position is in P, R or D and any of the following conditions is met.*1

  • The deck light switch is in the door position, and any of the doors or the tail gate is open.*2
  • A door is unlocked when the deck light switch is in the door position, the ignition switch is off, and all doors and the tail gate are closed.*3
  • The deck light switch is ON.

The cargo lights and bed lights illuminate when the shift position is in N, R or 1st gear and any of the following conditions is met.*4

  • The deck light switch is in the door position, and any of the doors or the tail gate is open.*5
  • A door is unlocked when the deck light switch is in the door position, the ignition switch is off, and all doors and the tail gate are closed.*3
  • The deck light switch is ON.

Turn Off

The cargo lights and bed lights turn off when the deck light switch is in the door position and any of the following conditions is satisfied:

  • The ignition switch is turned ACC or ON with all doors and the tail gate closed.
  • All doors are locked while the ignition switch is off and all doors and the tail gate are closed.

The cargo lights and bed lights turn off when the deck light switch is off.

Battery Saving

When there are no changes in the condition of the doors for 20 minutes, the cargo lights and bed lights will turn off.

HINT:

*1: Models with automatic transmission

*2: The light will not illuminate if the shift position is in D with the ignition switch ON and only the tail gate open.

*3: The light will turn off after approximately 15 seconds if there is no change in the vehicle condition.

*4: Models with manual transmission

*5: The light will not illuminate if the shift position is in 1st gear with the ignition switch ON and only the tail gate open.
