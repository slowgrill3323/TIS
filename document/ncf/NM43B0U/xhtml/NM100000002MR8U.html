HYBRID CONTROL

CONSTRUCTION

(a) The hybrid system uses motive force provided by the engine and the MG. The system optimally combines these forces in accordance with various driving conditions.

(b) The hybrid vehicle control ECU assembly constantly monitors the SOC of the HV battery, the HV battery temperature and the electrical load condition, and the ECM constantly monitors the engine coolant temperature. If any one of the monitoring items fails to meet requirements when the READY indicator is on or if the vehicle is driven in reverse, the hybrid vehicle control ECU assembly requests an engine start to drive the MG in order to charge the HV supply battery assembly.

(c) The hybrid system drives the vehicle by optimally combining the operation of the engine and the MG in accordance with the driving conditions listed in the table below

OPERATION

(a) Driving Condition A: On (READY) State

(1) Even if the driver turns the ignition switch ON (READY), sometimes the engine will not start. If this happens, the engine and the MG remain stopped. The engine will only start if conditions such as engine coolant temperature, SOC of the HV battery, HV battery temperature and electrical load require an engine start.

(2) After driving, if the driver stops the vehicle and moves the shift position to P, the ECM will continue to operate the engine. The engine will continue to operate until SOC of the HV battery engine coolant temperature, HV battery temperature and/or electrical load conditions reach a specified level.

(3) If any item monitored by the hybrid vehicle control ECU assembly and ECM indicates that the engine must be started when the READY indicator light is illuminated and the shift position is in P, the ECM connects the hybrid module clutch, activates the MG and starts the engine.

(f) Driving Condition E: During Deceleration

(1) While the vehicle is being driven with the shift position in D and it decelerates, the wheels drive the MG, and the MG operate as generators to charge the HV supply battery assembly. While the MG are operating as generators, they create a resistance to rotation at the wheels, producing a braking effect.
