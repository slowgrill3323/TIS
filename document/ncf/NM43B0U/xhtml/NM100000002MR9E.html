CENTER DIFFERENTIAL

CONSTRUCTION

(a) A torque-sensitive TORSEN LSD* is used in the center differential to generate differential limit force based on driving conditions and optimize torque distribution between the front/rear wheels. As a result, vehicle stability is enhanced when accelerating or driving at high-speeds, and turning performance is improved.

HINT:

*: TORSEN is a registered trademark of JTEKT Corporation.

(b) The TORSEN LSD is composed of a sun gear, a ring gear, 6 pinion gears, a planetary carrier and clutch plates. The sun gear is connected on the front wheel side, while the ring gear is connected on the rear wheel side.

(c) Sliding the center differential lock sleeve engages with the spline along the outer circumference of the center differential case will switch to free-lock for the center differential case.

OPERATION

(a) LSD Operation Description

(1) The TORSEN LSD generates differential limit force based on two operating principles: the operation of the clutch plate and the rotation resistance of the pinion gear. It lessens the difference in the rotational speed of the front/rear wheels and distributes torque from the high-speed side to the low-speed side

(2) The operation of the clutch plate generates a difference in the rotation speed of the sun gear (front wheel) and ring gear (rear wheel). Differential limit force is generated when each gear is moved in the axial direction by spiral-shaped gear teeth and each clutch plate operates.

(b) Operation when Front Wheel Speed = Rear Wheel Speed

(1) No differential limit force is generated when there is no difference in rotational speed between the front/rear wheels (such as when driving straight). Torque will therefore be distributed between the front/rear wheels at a ratio of 40:60 (front: rear) due to the difference in radius of the sun gear and ring gear.

(c) Operation when Front Wheel Speed > Rear Wheel Speed

(1) A differential limit force is generated by the difference in rotational speed between the sun gear and ring gear when the front wheel speed is high (such as during turning). As a result, the torque distribution to the front wheels will be reduced when accelerating during turning to suppress the front wheel skid tendency. Distribution will also change so as to suppress the front wheel speed versus input from the front wheels when decelerating during turning.

(d) Operation when Front Wheel Speed < Rear Wheel Speed

(1) A differential limit force is generated by the difference in rotational speed between the sun gear and ring gear when the rear wheel speed is high (such as during rear wheel slipping). As a result, the torque distribution to the rear wheels will be reduced when accelerating to suppress rear wheel slipping. Distribution will also change so as to suppress the rear wheel speed versus input from the rear wheels when decelerating.

(e) Free-lock Switching of Center Differential

(1) The front drive clutch sleeve slides to the position where the center differential case and transfer front drive gear piece are engaged. The center differential becomes locked when the center differential case and transfer drive sprocket are connected.
