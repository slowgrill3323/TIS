VALVE MECHANISM

CONSTRUCTION

(a) A Dual Variable Valve Timing-intelligent (Dual VVT-i) system (a continuously variable valve timing mechanism for intake and exhaust) is used which controls the intake and exhaust camshafts to provide optimal valve timing in accordance with the driving conditions.

(b) The No. 1 valve rocker arm sub-assembly is used as a valve mechanism and by achieving size reduction while drastically reducing the amount of friction that occurs between the sliding parts and cams, low fuel consumption is achieved. Also, an oil pressure type valve lash adjuster assembly is used to make valve clearance adjustment unnecessary in consideration of serviceability.

(c) A valve compression spring, whose upper portion is shaped like a beehive, is used to reduce inertial mass. As a result, the load on the valve compression spring and friction are reduced.

(d) The structure of the oil retainer of the valve lash adjuster assembly has been enhanced to achieve a compact and lightweight design.

(e) To withstand high exhaust temperatures, hollow axis exhaust valves containing sodium which has a low melting point and high heat transfer ability is used, increasing the cooling performance of the valves and ensuring reliability. Furthermore, by using hollow valves, weight is reduced, resulting in reduced friction.
