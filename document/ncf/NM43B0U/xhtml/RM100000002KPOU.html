Blind Spot Monitor Sensor LH/RH
  • Outputs radar waves from the blind spot monitor sensor to the blind spot sensor detection area, uses the reflected radar waves for detecting the presence of a vehicle, the vehicle-to-vehicle distance, and the relative speed, and then sends this information to the built-in signal processing circuit.
  • The signal processing circuit determines if a vehicle is present, and illuminates or blinks the outer rear view mirror indicator light accordingly.
  • Receives display dimmer signals from the main body ECU and dims the outer rear view mirror indicator light.

Outer Rear View Mirror Assembly LH/RH

  • Outer Rear View Mirror Indicator Light
  • Illuminates to inform the driver that a vehicle has been detected in the blind spot monitor sensor detection area.
  • Flashes to inform the driver when the turn signal switch is operated if a vehicle has been detected in the blind spot monitor sensor detection area.
  • Both indicator lights flash to inform the driver that a vehicle has been detected in the blind spot detection area while the driver is reversing.

Combination Meter Assembly

  • Driving Assist Information Indicator
  • Multi-information Display
  • Meter Buzzer
  • Sends the turn light signal to the blind spot monitor sensor RH.
  • Sends the on/off signal of the BSM function and RCTA function*1 to the blind spot monitor sensor RH.
  • Sends the customization information signal of the BSM function and RCTA function*1 to the blind spot monitor sensor RH.
  • Information such as the trailer connection status and trailer length are displayed on the multi-information display.
  • The method used by TLD to detect the trailer length can be changed to either "Auto" or "Manual" on the multi-information display. Detected information can also be registered multiple times.
  • Illuminates the driving assist information indicator to inform the driver when the BSM function is off.
  • Illuminates the driving assist information indicator to inform the driver when the RCTA function is off.*1
  • Sounds when a vehicle is detected in the blind spot monitor sensor detection area while the driver is reversing.
  • When a malfunction is detected in the blind spot monitor system or the blind spot monitor system determines that control is not possible, the driving assist information indicator illuminates and a message is displayed on the multi-information display to warn the driver.

Radio and Display Receiver Assembly

OPERATING CONDITION

(a) The BSM function operates when all of the following conditions are met:

(1) The BSM function is on.

(2) The shift position is other than R.

(3) The vehicle speed is greater than approximately 10 km/h (6 mph).

(b) The RCTA function operates when all of the following conditions are met: (Models with RCTA function)

(1) The RCTA function is on.

(2) The shift position is R.

(3) The speed of this vehicle is less than approximately 15 km/h (9 mph).

(4) The speed of the target vehicle is between approximately 8 km/h (5 mph) and 56 km/h (34 mph).

(c) The TMW function operates when all of the following conditions are met:

(1) All operating conditions for the BSM function are met.

(2) The trailer connection status is detected by the ATD function.

(3) A supported trailer is selected (Conventional hitch type and length less or equal to 12.0 m (39 ft.)) or when the trailer is detected by TLD function and user selects hitch type as conventional and detected trailer length is less or equal to 12.0 m (39 ft.).

(d) The ATD function operates when all of the following conditions are met:

(1) The vehicle and trailer are connected.

(2) Ignition switch is turned ON.

(3) The shift position is in P or R and the vehicle is stopped for approximately 90 seconds or more.

HINT:

When the vehicle is stopped and then travels straight ahead or turns at a speed of 1.08 km (0.67 mph) or higher and there are no other vehicles, etc. nearby (such as when vehicles are not on both sides of own vehicle in a parking area, when not in a garage, or when trailers are not on both sides of own vehicle at a camping ground), ATD detects the trailer within 10 seconds.

(b) RCTA Function (Models with RCTA Function)

(1) The RCTA function can detect vehicles in its detection areas.

  • The system continuously measures the relative speed of an approaching vehicle and its distance. If it is determined that the approaching vehicle will cross the path of this vehicle, the Estimated Crossing Time (ECT) is calculated. When the ECT is 2.5 seconds or less, the system alerts the driver by flashing the outer rear view mirror indicator lights and sounding the RCTA buzzer.

(d) ATD Function

(1) Trailers that use trailer brake control ECU can be detected by using the 7-pin trailer brake connectors.

(2) Other types of trailers can be detected using the blind spot monitor sensor.

(3) When a trailer is detected via trailer brake control ECU or blind spot monitor sensor, RCTA function will be disabled.

(4) As soon as the trailer is detected, the detection area will be extended to a maximum length of approximately 15 m (16 ft.) from the rear bumper of the truck.

(5) If the trailer does not have a 7-pin connector, or the 7-pin connector is not engaged, the trailer is detected via the BSM sensor. In this case parking support brake system* and intuitive parking assist functions will not be disabled.

  • *: Models with parking support brake system

(e) TLD Function

(1) When a trailer has been detected by the ATD function and "Auto" is selected on the multi-information display in the combination meter assembly, the left/right blind spot monitor sensor estimates the trailer length when the vehicle turns at least twice on a curve that is approximately 90° or higher. Once estimation is complete, the BSM detection area is adjusted based on the trailer length.

(2) If "Manual" is selected, the blind spot monitor sensor adjusts the BSM detection area based on the length entered on the multi-information display in the combination meter assembly.

FUNCTION

(a) Blind Spot Monitor System Operation

(1) The combination meter assembly customization setting can be used to turn the BSM function and RCTA function* on and off.

(2) A driving assist information indicator is located in the combination meter assembly to make it possible to check that the BSM function is off.

(3) A driving assist information indicator is located in the combination meter assembly to make it possible to check that the RCTA function is off.*

(4) The combination meter assembly customization setting can be used to change the brightness of the outer rear view mirror indicator light, volume of the RCTA buzzer* and the BSM function detection timing.

(5) The method used by TLD to detect the trailer length can be changed to either "Auto" or "Manual" on the multi-information display in the combination meter assembly. Detected information can also be registered multiple times.

  • *: Models with RCTA function

(b) Customization Function

(1) Settings of some functions can be changed as desired by the user. Refer to the Repair Manual for details.

DIAGNOSIS

(a) In order to make system inspections easier to perform, a diagnosis function is used in consideration of serviceability. The DTCs of malfunctioning parts can be read by connecting the Global TechStream (GTS). Refer to the Repair Manual for details.
