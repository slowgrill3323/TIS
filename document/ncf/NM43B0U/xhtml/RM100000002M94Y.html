GENERAL

OUTLINE

(a) A Dual USB-C power outlet socket has been provided on the instrument panel upper tray. Additionally, a Dual USB-C power outlet socket has been provided on the console rear end panel sub-assembly, depending on the model.

(b) A DC 12V power outlet socket has been provided on the instrument panel upper tray and rear end panel sub-assembly, depending on the model.

(c) An AC 120V power outlet socket has been provided on the console rear end panel, depending on the model.

(d) The main switch that enables the accessory power outlet system (120 VAC) to be turned ON/OFF, is located on the driver side instrument panel. Also, the main switch assembly has an indicator light in consideration of usability.

PRECAUTION

CAUTION:

Observe the following precautions. If the precautions are not observed, an accident resulting in death or serious injury may occur.

  • Do not use devices such as the following when driving. Also, do not use a device if it cannot be secured within the vehicle.
    • Devices which may distract the driver and be a hindrance to safe driving, such as a TV, DVD player, etc.
    • Unsecured devices or devices which generate heat, such as a toaster, microwave, electric heater, electric kettle, coffee maker, etc., as they may fly around possibly causing injury, burns or a fire in the case of sudden braking or an accident.
    • Devices which may fall under the pedals and prevent the brake pedal from being depressed, such as a hair dryer, AC adapter, mouse, etc.

NOTICE:

Observe the following precautions. Failure to do so may lead to the power outlets not operating correctly or damage to the vehicle or a connected device.

  • To avoid short circuit or malfunction
    • Do not set a toaster or other device which generates heat near the interior components or on a seat. Heat may cause these parts to melt or burn.
    • Do not use devices which are sensitive to vibration or heat in the vehicle. These devices may malfunction due to vibration while driving or heat while the vehicle is parked in the sun.
    • When not using a power outlet, make sure to close the cover. If foreign matter or a liquid enters the power outlet, it may cause a malfunction or short circuit.
    • Do not use a multi-point outlet adapter as doing so may overload the power outlet.
  • When the ambient temperature is high
    • If the temperature inside the vehicle is high, such as after the vehicle has been parked in the sun, use the air conditioning system to sufficiently ventilate or cool the interior.
  • When the ambient temperature is low
    • If the vehicle has been stopped in a cold area, to protect the HV battery, the power outlets may not be able to be used. In this case, drive the vehicle for a while to warm up the HV battery.
  • To prevent depletion of the HV battery
    • If a device with a power consumption of 100 W or more is to be used for an extended period of time, turn off all electrical devices and accessories (E.g. headlights, air conditioner, etc.).

(a) Accessory power outlet system (2400 W power outlet socket)

  • Make sure that all devices connected across all accessory power outlets (AC 120 V/2400 W) have a maximum combined power consumption of 2400 W or less. If the power consumption of all devices connected across all accessory power outlets exceeds the rated capacity, the protection function will operate and the accessory power outlet system will not be able to be used.
  • Depending on the device to be used, the current flow may be high and the initial peak wattage may exceed 2400 W. In this case, the protection circuit may operate, and the power outlets may not be able to be used.
  • Depending on the connected device, it may cause interference with TV and radio broadcasts.
  • The voltage of the power outlets cannot be measured with a commercially available electrical tester.

    CAUTION:

    Do not perform an inspection of the output voltage as the accessory power outlet system uses high voltage supplied by the HV battery.

  • When using the accessory power outlet system, the noise of a cooling fan may be heard from under the center console. This is not a malfunction.

(b) Devices that may not operate correctly (2400 W power outlet socket)

(1) The following 120 V devices may not operate properly even if their power consumption is less than 2400 W:

  • Devices with high initial peak wattage
  • Measuring devices that process precise data
  • Devices that require an extremely stable power supply
  • Devices that require a constant power supply from the power outlet, such as a device with a timer
