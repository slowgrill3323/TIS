FUEL PUMP (FOR HIGH PRESSURE)

CONSTRUCTION

(a) A high-efficiency single plunger type is used to reduce driving torque.

(b) Consists of an electromagnetic spill control valve that adjusts the amount of discharged high pressure fuel, a plunger that is operated by the exhaust camshaft to pressurize the fuel, a check valve that mechanically opens and closes the path to the fuel delivery pipe sub-assembly (for direct injection) and fuel relief valve that leaks fuel at a high pressure fuel system malfunction.

(c) A roller lifter (fuel pump lifter assembly) is used to operate the plunger, supporting higher output and higher fuel pressure.

(d) Installed to the No. 5 camshaft bearing cap, and is operated by a cam installed to the exhaust camshaft.

(e) The flow control valve (FCV) is built into the fuel tube sub-assembly of the low pressure fuel inlet and is used to dampen high pressure fluctuations (pulses) from fuel discharge from the fuel (engine room side) pump assembly (for high pressure side). With this, the fuel pressure pulsation damper built into the fuel (engine room side) pump assembly (for high pressure side) has been made compact and lighter.

(f) The high pressure fuel pressure is variably controlled between 2.4 and 30 MPa according to driving conditions to reduce friction losses.

(g) A metal taper seal is used for the fuel pipe (for high pressure side) fastener.

OPERATION

(a) During the exhaust camshaft portion of the pump cycle, the spill control valve is opened, and the pump plunger (piston) is moved downward by spring force. This allows fuel to be drawn into the cylinder of the pump. If the spill control valve has not been closed yet, when the cam forces the plunger to move upward, the fuel in the pump cylinder (this fuel is not pressurized) will be pushed back to the pump inlet (fuel tank side).

(b) In order to close the spill control valve as the piston is moving upward, the ECM sends a signal to the valve. When the spill control valve is closed and the plunger is moving upward, the pressure in the pump cylinder will rise. As this pressure rises above 60 kPa (or the pressure of the delivery pipe, whichever is higher), the fuel will begin to flow to the fuel delivery pipe sub-assembly (for direct injection). The ECM calculates the target fuel pressure based on driving conditions. The ECM controls the pressure by operating the spill control valve. The timing and duration of the spill control valve closing are varied to cause the pump pressure to meet the target pressure.

(c) If the fuel pressure in the high-pressure fuel pipes is abnormally high, the fuel relief valve discharges some of fuel in order to limit the pressure.
