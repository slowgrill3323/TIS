Detects the amount of twist of the torsion bar. Based on the torque applied to the torsion bar, the sensor creates an electrical signal, and sends this signal to the power steering ECU.

Reduces the speed of the power steering motor and transmits the rotation of the power steering motor to the rack shaft.

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) The power steering ECU calculates the steering assist force based on steering torque information from the steering torque sensor, vehicle speed information from the skid control ECU*1 or No. 2 skid control ECU*2 and information from each ECU and controls the power steering motor to generate the required steering assist torque.

HINT:

*1: Gasoline models

*2: HEV models

Steering Assist Control

Basic Control

Calculates the assist current required based on the steering torque value and the vehicle speed and operates the power steering motor.

Compensation Control

Compensates with a force, according to the driver's steering maneuver, improving the comfort of steering.

Sport Mode Control*1

When drive mode SPORT*2 or SPORT S/SPORT S+*3 is selected, the power steering ECU switches to assist characteristics with higher steering responsiveness than NORMAL mode to achieve a more direct steering feeling.

Multi-terrain Select Cooperative Control*4

When multi-terrain select is selected, it provides the optimum steering assist for off-road driving and improves the steering feeling.

Tow Haul Mode Select Cooperative Control*5

When TOW HAUL is selected, it provides the optimum steering assist for towing and improves the steering feeling.

Brake Control System Cooperative Control

Receives requests from the skid control ECU*6 or No. 2 skid control ECU*7 and performs steering assist control in accordance with the operating condition of the brake control system. This facilitates the steering operation of the driver, achieving a high level of vehicle stability.

Driving Assistance System Request Control

The power steering ECU operates the steering wheel and provides steering assistance using various driving assistance systems. For details, see each driving assistance system section.

Power Supply Voltage Drop Control

When the power steering ECU detects a power supply voltage drop, it sends a load control demand to the air conditioning amplifier assembly in order to limit electrical usage.

FAIL-SAFE

(a) If the power steering ECU detects a malfunction in the EPS system, the power steering ECU illuminates the power steering warning light (red indicator*1 and yellow indicator*2) and displays the warning message on the multi-information display, and sounds the buzzer to alert the driver of the malfunction.

HINT:

*1: Illuminates when a malfunction which results in a loss of steering assist torque.

*2: Illuminates when a malfunction which limits the steering assist torque.

(b) If a system malfunction occurs, the power steering ECU changes the control mode to fail-safe mode. For details, refer to the Repair Manual.

DIAGNOSIS

(a) The power steering ECU will also store a Diagnostic Trouble Code (DTC). The DTC can be accessed through the use of the Global TechStream (GTS). For details, refer to the Repair Manual.
