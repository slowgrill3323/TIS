(b) Daytime Running Light System

(1) The daytime running light system is designed to automatically illuminate the Light Emitting Diode (LED) lights during the daytime to keep the vehicle highly visible to other vehicles.

(c) Light Auto Turn-off System

(1) The light auto turn-off system is used to prevent the driver from leaving the vehicle with the headlights, parking lights, front side marker lights, front fog lights*, taillights, rear side marker lights, or license plate lights on.

HINT:

*: Models with fog light assembly

(d) Manual Headlight Beam Level Control System

(1) The manual headlight beam level control system keeps the beam of the headlight adjusted to the appropriate level in accordance with the number (weight) of passengers and volume of luggage.

(e) Automatic Light Control System

(1) When the light control switch is in the AUTO position, the automatic light control sensor detects the ambient light level and automatically turns the headlights, parking lights, front side marker lights, taillights, rear side marker lights, and license plate lights on or off accordingly.

(f) Welcome Light Illumination System

(1) The welcome light illumination system operates the turn signal lights, parking lights, front side marker lights, taillights, rear side marker lights, and license plate lights in conjunction with the locking and unlocking of the doors, ensuring nighttime usability.

(g) Headlight On/Off Reminder Control system

(1) When the user forgets to turn the headlights on or off, a warning message is displayed on the multi-information display in the combination meter assembly.

(h) Wiper-linked Low Beam Headlight Control

(1) Wiper-linked low beam headlight control is designed to automatically illuminate the low beam headlights when it is raining to make the vehicle more visible to other vehicles.

(i) Collision-linked Control System

(1) When a collision occurs where the SRS airbags are deployed, the turn signal lights are flashed to inform people near the vehicle of its location.

(j) Sequential Turn Signal Lights (Models with Sequential Turn Signal Light)

(1) The turn signal lights illuminate in sequence, intuitively conveying the driver's intention to change course.

(k) Lane Change Flasher System

(1) The lane change flasher system flashes the turn signal light a certain number of times when the turn signal switch is moved to the lane change position. By using the lane change flasher system when changing lanes, it is no longer necessary to hold the turn signal switch in the lane change position.

(l) Cargo Compartment Light Control

(1) Cargo lights and bed lights are provided to enhance usability. They are designed to light up the rear deck of the vehicle and should only be operated during cargo loading/unloading.

(m) Trailer Hitch Light Control (Models with Trailer Hitch Light)

(1) An additional light on the tail gate of the truck which will provide the vehicle with the lighting needed when the trailer backup guide feature is being activated.* The trailer hitch light will also turn on when in reverse to assist with vision and when the cargo lights are on.

  • *: Models with trailer backup guide

(n) LED Illumination System (Models with Center LED Illumination Light)

(1) Center LED illumination light that can be turned on and off using a switch when the high beam headlights are illuminated is provided.

(2) When the fog lights are illuminated, the fog light color (white/yellow) can be changed by operating the switch.*

  • *: Models with trailhunter package

(3) The light emitting diode illumination switch and the fog light color switch are installed to the bottom left part of the instrument panel.
