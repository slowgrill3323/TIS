Parking Assist ECU
  • Combines video signals from the front, rear, left and right television camera to create a seamless overhead image.
  • Calculates each guide line based on information signals from each ECU and sensor, combines the lines with the panoramic view and images from each television camera assembly, and outputs a video signal to the multi-display.
  • Turns each television camera assembly on and off according to the input shift position signal and each switch signal.
  • Combines detection information with the currently displayed multi-terrain monitor system screen according to signals from the clearance warning ECU assembly, blind spot monitor sensor RH and outputs a video signal to the multi-display.

Radio and Display Receiver Assembly

Displays image information received from the parking assist ECU, and allows the display mode to be changed by pressing the buttons on the screen.

(e) Manual Display Mode and Automatic Display Mode

(1) Manual Display Mode

  • Manual display mode is a method of displaying the multi-terrain monitor system screen according to the needs of the driver via operation of the panoramic view monitor switch and touch.
  • The multi-terrain monitor system is displayed by operating the panoramic view monitor switch when vehicle speed is approximately 16 km/h (10 mph) or less.
  • When the vehicle speed exceeds approximately 16 km/h (10 mph), the multi-terrain monitor system screen ends and switches to display the navigation screen or information screen.

(2) Automatic Display Mode

  • When starting off and stopping the vehicle in automatic display mode, it is possible to display the "panoramic view and front view" automatically instead of by pressing the panoramic view monitor switch.
  • The automatic display mode can be set with the auto mode button displayed on the screen.

(3) Display Function Linked to Intuitive Parking Assist System

  • When the intuitive parking assist system detects a stationary object, the monitor screen automatically changes from the current screen (navigation screen, DVD screen, etc.) to the clearance information screen with the "panoramic view and front view" screen.
  • When all of the following conditions are met, the panoramic view and front view will be displayed.
    • The shift position is not P or R.
    • The intuitive parking assist system has detected a stationary object.
  • When either of the following conditions is met, the panoramic view and front view will be canceled and return to its original screen.
    • The intuitive parking assist system does not detect a stationary object.

FUNCTION

(a) Multi-terrain Monitor Screen and Operation

  • Each screen of the multi-terrain monitor displays guide lines, estimated course lines calculated from steering angle signals input from the steering sensor via CAN communication, etc., together according to the selected screen display mode to inform the driver of the tire positions and trajectories which cannot be directly seen from the driver seat.
  • When the intuitive parking assist system or RCTA function detects an operation target, intuitive parking assist system information or RCTA function information is superimposed on the screen according to the selected screen display mode.

(1) Front View and Dual Side Views

  • Images from the front television camera assembly and the left and right side television camera assemblies are displayed simultaneously to help the driver in operations such as checking conditions near the vehicle front and the travel direction of the wheels.
  • The front view and dual side views are displayed when the front view and dual side views mode button is selected, or when the panoramic view monitor switch is pressed while the navigation screen or information setting screen is displayed.

(2) Under Vehicle Terrain View and Dual Side Views

  • Images from the front television camera assembly and the left and right side television camera assemblies are displayed simultaneously to help the driver in operations such as checking conditions under the vehicle and understanding the front wheel positions.
  • The under vehicle terrain view and dual side views are displayed by selecting the under vehicle terrain view and dual side views mode button.

    CAUTION:

    • Guide Lines
      • The tire shape lines and vehicle shape lines may differ from actual vehicle positions depending on the number of passengers, cargo weight, road grade, road surface conditions, brightness of the surrounding environment, installed options, installation of non-specified tires or suspension, etc. Always drive while confirming the safety of your surroundings.
    • Display of Under Vehicle Terrain View
      • The displayed image is an image captured in the past. Therefore, the displayed under vehicle terrain view does not necessarily match the current conditions; for example, if an object moves after the image is captured. Also, it may not be possible to confirm obstacles in the image at night or in other dark environments.

    HINT:

    • In the following cases, the under vehicle terrain view display turns black.
      • Vehicle has not been driven for a certain distance or longer after the engine starts or the system returns to normal
      • Vehicle has not been driven for a certain distance or longer after theengine starts or the system returns to normal (gasoline models)
      • Vehicle has not been driven for a certain distance or longer after thehybrid system starts or the system returns to normal (HEV models)
      • Tires have spun
      • When ABS is operating
      • When the system does not operate normally
    • The system may not operate normally in the following situations.
      • Snow-covered road
      • When shadows are cast by a light source such as sunlight or artificial illumination
      • On a slippery road or when the wheels spin
      • When there is dirt or other foreign matter on the camera lens
      • Watery surface (river, sea, etc.)
      • Optional equipment has been installed
      • When the camera is covered or an object is in the imaging range
      • Tires have been replaced
      • When the camera is not in the correct position, such as when the tailgate is open
      • When the steering wheel is operated for more than a certain steering angle or when on a gradient, such as a slope
    • Images captured in the past are displayed. Therefore, the screen may differ from actual conditions in the following situations.
      • When the situation changes after the image is captured, such as an object moves or enters the range
      • When objects such as sand or snow collapse or move
      • When the obstacle moves
      • There is a puddle, tract of mud, etc., within the display range
      • The vehicle slips

(8) Moving View

  • This view combines images from the front, rear, left and right side television camera assemblies to display an image of the vehicle from a viewpoint diagonally above the vehicle. The displayed image rotates 360 degrees around the vehicle displayed on the screen to assist the driver to confirm the safety of the surroundings. Furthermore, after the image of the vehicle from a viewpoint diagonally above the vehicle rotates a full 360 degrees, an overhead view of the vehicle is displayed.
  • Using the rotation pause button, the rotation of the moving view can be paused and unpaused at will.
  • When all of the following conditions are met, the moving view will be displayed.
    • The shift position is P.*1
    • The parking brake is applied.*2
    • The shift position is not R.*2
    • The vehicle is stopped.*2
    • Intuitive parking assist system is on.
    • Panoramic view monitor switch is pressed.
