CHARGING CONTROL

FUNCTION OF MAIN COMPONENTS

Component

Function

ECM
  • Detects the electrical load.
  • Monitors the battery condition based on the current, battery temperature, state of charge, state of health and charge capacity detected by the battery state sensor assembly.
  • Calculates the request voltage based on the electrical load, state of the battery and various sensors, and sends the target voltage signal to the IC regulator.
  • Controls the voltage generated by the generator assembly.

Generator Assembly

IC Regulator

Receives target voltage command signals from the ECM and charges the battery.

Battery State Sensor Assembly

Detects the current, voltage, temperature, state of charge, state of health and startability of the battery and outputs the data to the ECM via LIN communication.

Battery

Supplies power to the electrical components such as the blower motor, wiper, lights and each ECU.

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) Basic Control Mode

(1) The ECM decreases the charging voltage during acceleration and increases the charging voltage during deceleration to optimize power generation by the generator assembly. As a result, engine load is reduced and fuel economy is enhanced.

(2) During idling and when driving at a constant speed, the ECM adjusts the generated voltage to bring the current integrated value* closer to the target value.

HINT:

*: Integrated value of the battery input/output current as detected by the battery state sensor assembly.

(b) Constant Voltage Mode

(1) In the following situations, basic control is temporarily suspended and constant voltage control is performed.

  1. Stable use the battery
    1. To prevent battery plates from forming lead sulfate due to repeated charging and discharging, constant voltage control is performed for every 20 hours of driving to sufficiently charge the battery.
    2. Constant voltage control is performed when the state of charge of the battery is low or the temperature of the battery is high/low to minimize deterioration of the battery.
