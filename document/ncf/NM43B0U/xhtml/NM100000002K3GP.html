Canister

Contains activated charcoal to absorb the vapors that are created in the fuel tank assembly as well as during refueling.

Opens in accordance with the signals from the ECM when the system is purging in order to send the fuel vapor that was absorbed by the canister into the intake manifold. In system monitoring mode, this valve controls the introduction of vacuum into the fuel tank assembly.

ECM

  • The ECM monitors the system for any leaks and outputs a DTC if a malfunction is found.

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) Fuel Vapor-containment System

(1) Parked (Power Switch Mode Off)

  • The ECM seals the fuel vapors in the fuel tank assembly by closing the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly). At this time, if the internal pressure of the fuel tank assembly increases to a level above the specified pressure of the fuel outlet valve located in the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly), the fuel outlet valve opens to release the internal pressure of the fuel tank assembly. This prevents the internal pressure in the fuel tank assembly from increasing abnormally.

(2) Refueling Wait

  • When the fuel lid opener switch is operated, the ECM opens the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly), allowing the fuel vapors to be absorbed by the canister and reducing the pressure in the fuel tank assembly. The fuel lid will not open unless the internal pressure of the fuel tank assembly decreases to a level close to atmospheric pressure. In addition, the multi-information display will show the "Please Wait Fuel Door Opening".

(3) Refueling Ready

  • When the internal pressure of the fuel tank assembly decreases to a level close to atmospheric pressure, the ECM opens the fuel lid. At this time, the multi-information display will show "Ready to Refuel". Because the ECM keeps the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) open during refueling, the fuel vapors that are created during refueling pass through the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) and are absorbed by the canister. When the fuel lid is closed after refueling, the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) closes in order to seal the fuel tank assembly.
  • If 30 minutes elapse after the fuel lid is opened, the ECM closes the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) to seals the fuel tank assembly in order to prevent the fuel vapors from escaping, even if the fuel lid is not closed.

(b) Purge Flow Control

(1) When the engine has reached predetermined parameters (closed loop, engine coolant temperature above 70°C (158°F), etc.), stored fuel vapors are purged from the canister whenever the purge VSV is opened by the ECM. The ECM changes the duty ratio cycle of the purge VSV thus controlling purge flow volume. The purge flow volume depends on the intake manifold pressure and the duty ratio cycle of the purge VSV. Atmospheric pressure is allowed into the canister to ensure that purge flow is constantly maintained whenever purge vacuum is applied to the canister.

(2) Due to the adoption of a fuel vapor-containment system, purge flow control occurs following 2 patterns, depending on the internal pressure of the fuel tank assembly.

(3) If the internal pressure in the fuel tank assembly is near atmospheric pressure, the ECM will not open the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) even if the purge VSV is open. At this time, the fuel vapors that are absorbed in the canister are drawn inside the engine and burned.

(c) EVAP Leak Check

(1) The following are the typical conditions necessary to enable an evaporative emission leak check:

*: If the engine coolant temperature does not drop below 35°C (95°F), this time is extended to 7 hours. After that, if the temperature is not less than 35°C (95°F), the time is extended to 9.5 hours.

Typical Enabling Conditions
  • 5 hours have elapsed after the engine has been turned off.*
  • Altitude: Below 2400 m (8000 ft.)
  • Auxiliary Battery Voltage: 10.5 V or higher
  • Ignition Switch: Off
  • Engine Coolant Temperature: Between 4.4 and 35°C (40 and 95°F)
  • Intake Air Temperature: Between 4.4 and 35°C (40 and 95°F)

HINT:

The canister pump module performs the fuel evaporative emission leak check. This check is done approximately 5 hours after the engine is turned off. A sound may be heard coming from underneath the luggage compartment for several minutes. This sound does not indicate a malfunction.

(2) The ECM performs evaporative emission leak checks using 2 types of judgment methods.

  • When the internal pressure in the fuel tank assembly is equal to atmospheric pressure:
    • The ECM actuates the leak detection pump to introduce a vacuum into the EVAP system pipes and the canister, in order to check for leaks and check the function of parts.
    • The ECM actuates the leak detection pump and opens the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) to introduce a vacuum into the fuel tank assembly, in order to check the fuel tank assembly for leaks and determine whether the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) is stuck closed.

(3) Atmospheric Pressure Measurement

  1. When the ignition switch is turned off, the purge VSV and the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) are off (closed). Therefore, atmospheric pressure is introduced into the canister.
  2. The ECM measures the atmospheric pressure through the signals provided by the canister pressure sensor.
  3. If the measurement value is out of standard, the ECM actuates the leak detection pump in order to monitor the pressure changes.
  4. If the ECM does not detect pressure changes, it stops the leak check and stores DTC (Diagnostic Trouble Codes) in its memory.

(4) First Reference Pressure Measurement

  1. The ECM turns the vent valve off (vent), introducing atmospheric pressure into the canister, and actuates the leak detection pump, in order to create negative pressure vacuum.
  2. At this time, the pressure will not decrease beyond a certain level due to the atmospheric pressure that enters through the reference orifice.
  3. The ECM compares the logic value and this pressure and stores the measured pressure as the first reference pressure.
  4. If the measurement value is below the standard (too much vacuum), the ECM determines that the reference orifice is clogged and stores DTC in its memory.
  5. If the measurement value is above the standard (pressure is too high, not enough vacuum), the ECM determines that a high flow rate of pressure is passing through the reference orifice and stores DTC in memory.

(5) EVAP Leak Check

  1. While actuating the leak detection pump, the ECM turns the vent valve on (closes the vent) in order to introduce a vacuum into the canister (the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) is closed as shown below).
  2. When the pressure in the system stabilizes, the ECM compares this pressure and the first reference pressure in order to determine if a leak is occurring.
  3. If the detection value is below the first reference pressure, the ECM determines that there is no leak.
  4. If the detection value is above the first reference pressure and near atmospheric pressure, the ECM determines that there is a large leak and stores DTC in its memory.
  5. If the detection value is above the first reference pressure and still below atmospheric pressure, the ECM determines that there is a small leak and stores DTC in its memory.
  6. When the fuel tank internal pressure equals atmospheric pressure, if there is fluctuation in the fuel tank pressure sensor (vapor pressure sensor) value, the ECM determines that the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) is stuck open and stores DTC in memory.

(6) Check Valve Monitoring

  2. If pressure does not change, the ECM determines the condition to be normal.
  3. If pressure changes, the ECM will stop the check valve monitoring and store DTC in its memory.

(8) Fuel Vapor-containment Valve (Close Tank Valve Assembly) Stuck Closed Judgment and Fuel Tank Leak Check

  1. When Fuel Tank Internal Pressure Equals Atmospheric Pressure
    1. The ECM actuates the leak detection pump, and turns on the vent valve (vent is closed) and the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) (valve opens), in order to introduce a vacuum into the fuel tank assembly, pipes, and canister.
    2. When the pressure in the system stabilizes, the ECM compares this pressure and the first reference pressure in order to determine if a leak is occurring.
    3. If the detection value is below the first reference pressure, the ECM determines that there is no leak.
    4. If the detection value is above the first reference pressure and near atmospheric pressure, the ECM determines that there is a large leak and stores DTC in its memory.
    5. If the detection value is above the first reference pressure and still below atmospheric pressure, the ECM determines that there is a small leak and stores DTC in its memory.
    6. At this time, if there is no fluctuation in the fuel tank pressure sensor (vapor pressure sensor) value, the ECM determines that the fuel vapor-containment valve (close tank valve assembly) is stuck closed and stores DTC in memory.
