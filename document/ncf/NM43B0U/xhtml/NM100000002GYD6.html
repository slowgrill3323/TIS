Title: T24A-FTS (ENGINE MECHANICAL): ENGINE UNIT: CYLINDER HEAD; 2024 - 2026 MY Tacoma Tacoma HV [12/2023 -        ]

CYLINDER HEAD

CONSTRUCTION

(a) The cylinder head structure is simplified by separating the cam journal portion (camshaft housing sub-assembly) from the cylinder head sub-assembly.

(b) A pent-roof combustion chamber is used with the direct fuel injector assembly and spark plug centered in the combustion chamber.

(c) By locating the spark plug in the fuel spray area of the direct fuel injector assembly, a high spray guide combustion stability is realized. Also, combustion is performed at a delayed angle, contributing to early catalyst start times and reducing the discharge of hazardous substances.

(d) A laser cladded valve seat is used, thus enabling the shape of the intake port to be optimized, increasing the tumble flow and intake air flow volume, improving engine power and fuel economy.

(e) The valve lash adjuster assemblies are installed in the upper part of the cylinder head sub-assembly, creating a structure where the oil passage supplying oil to the valve lash adjuster assemblies are combined with the interior of the cylinder head sub-assembly.

(f) An injector holder sub-assembly is used to locate the fuel delivery pipe sub-assembly (for direct injection) on top of the engine for the center injection system.

(g) The injector holder sub-assembly ensures holding and sealing performance of the fuel delivery pipe sub-assembly (for direct injection) and weight has been reduced by thinning of the material.

(h) By using an injector holder sub-assembly, a center injection construction has been established with different angles between the spark plug tube and fuel injector tube.

(i) The water jacket in the cylinder head sub-assembly is divided into an upper and lower jacket, with increased flow in the lower jacket to increase cooling performance of the combustion chamber, and the divider of the upper and lower water jacket reinforces the combustion chamber, suitable for high pressures and heat generation of a forced induction engine.

(j) By optimizing the shape of the water jacket, reducing combustion chamber temperatures, and minimizing differences in temperature between cylinders, a high-compression ratio can be realized, resulting in high torque, high output and reduced fuel consumption.
