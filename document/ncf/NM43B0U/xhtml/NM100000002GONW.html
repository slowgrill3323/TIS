TURBOCHARGER

CONSTRUCTION

(a) A turbocharger sub-assembly is used to achieve high engine output, high torque and low emissions.

(b) An efficient twin-scroll turbocharger is used in the turbocharger sub-assembly to prevent exhaust interference between cylinders and achieve high engine output and to high torque over all ranges from low to high engine speeds.

(c) A wastegate valve is used in the turbocharger sub-assembly. When the vehicle is being driven at low load (boost is not necessary), the wastegate valve opens to decrease the back pressure and reduce pumping losses during the exhaust stroke. Also, the wastegate valve opens during start-up to decrease the amount of time needed for the Three-Way Catalytic converter (TWC) to warm up.

(d) The wastegate valve opens to introduce high temperature gas directly to the Three-Way Catalytic converter (TWC). As a result, TWC warm-up performance during start-up and exhaust gas purification performance are enhanced.

OPERATION

(a) Wastegate Valve

(1) When the engine is stopped, the wastegate valve is open.

(2) When the vacuum regulating valve assembly cuts the vacuum to the actuator according to signals from the ECM, the actuator opens the wastegate valve through a link.
