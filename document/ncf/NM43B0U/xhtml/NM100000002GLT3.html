EMERGENCY DRIVING STOP SYSTEM CONTROL

OPERATING CONDITION

(a) When the following conditions are met, Emergency Driving Stop System (EDSS) is performed:

  • Dynamic radar cruise control is operating.
  • LTA is operating and the hands off steering wheel warning has continued for approximately 9 seconds or more.
  • The vehicle speed is approximately 50 km/h (30 mph) or more.
  • No system malfunctions are detected.

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) This system can be divided into 4 phases. When "warning phase 1" and "warning phase 2" are met, and driver warnings and speed restriction are being performed, normal/unresponsive driver operation judgement is performed. If the system judges that the driver is unresponsive, the vehicle is decelerated and stopped by "deceleration stop phase" and "stop hold phase" continues after the vehicle has stopped.

(b) Warning Phase 1

(1) When the lane tracing assist system is operating, the hands not on steering wheel warning has continued for approximately 9 seconds or more due to no steering operation, the meter buzzer sounds, a driver warning is displayed on the multi-information display and headup display* and normal/unresponsive driver operation judgement is performed.

  • *: Models with headup display

(2) When the lane centering function is operating and the driver has not provided steering input such as holding the steering wheel even though the warning has continued for approximately 15 seconds, the system transitions to warning phase 2.

(c) Warning Phase 2

(1) The meter buzzer sounds, a driver warning is displayed on the multi-information display and headup display*, and normal/abnormal driver operation judgement is performed.

  • *: Models with headup display

(2) The lane tracing assist lane centering function continues and the vehicle decelerates at a gentle rate to within a certain vehicle speed range.

(3) When the vehicle is decelerating, the brake lights may illuminate, depending on the road conditions.*

*: Except models for New Caledonia

(4) When deceleration control is being performed, the hazard lights flash*1 to warn surrounding vehicles of the emergency.*2

*1: When the vehicle is decelerating, the brake lights may illuminate, depending on the road conditions.

*2: Models for New Caledonia

(5) When the warning has continued for approximately 30 seconds or more, the vehicle has decelerated to a certain vehicle speed (approximately 50 km/h (30 mph)) and no operation has been performed by the driver, a driver unresponsive judgment is made and the vehicle transitions to the deceleration stop phase.

(d) Deceleration Stop Phase

(1) When a driver unresponsive judgment has been made and the vehicle has transitioned to the deceleration stop phase, the vehicle is stopped after gentle deceleration.

(2) Inside the vehicle, the situation is conveyed to the driver by sounding the meter buzzer and displaying on the multi-information display and headup display*1 and outside the vehicle, the stop lights illuminate.*2

  • *1: Models with headup display

    *2: Except models for New Caledonia

(3) Inside the vehicle, the situation is conveyed to the driver by sounding the meter buzzer and displaying on the multi-information display and outside the vehicle, the stop lights illuminate and the hazard lights flash of the emergency.*

  • *: Models for New Caledonia

(4) When the vehicle stops, the system transitions to stop hold phase.

(e) Stop Hold Phase

(1) After the vehicle stops, the vehicle is held stationary by automatic engagement of the electric parking brake system.

(2) Even though the stop lights turn off, the hazard lights continue to flash.*

  • *: Except models for New Caledonia

(3) Even though the stop lights turn off, the hazard lights continue to flash and the doors are automatically unlocked to provide assistance to the driver.*

  • *: Models for New Caledonia

(f) Function Cancellation Conditions during Operation (Warning Phase 1 / Warning Phase 2 / Deceleration Stop Phase)

(1) If the following conditions are detected, operation of the emergency driving stop system is canceled:

Function Cancellation Conditions

*: Models for New Caledonia
  • Lane tracing assist is canceled.
  • Dynamic radar cruise control is canceled.
  • The steering wheel is held.
  • The brake pedal is operated.
  • The accelerator pedal is operated.
  • A brake system malfunction is detected.
  • Hazard switch is operated.*

(g) Cancellation Conditions for Operating Function (Stop Hold Phase)

(1) If the following conditions are detected, operation of the emergency driving stop system is canceled:

Function Cancellation Conditions

  • The driving assist switch is operated when the shift position is in P.
  • The ignition switch is off.
  • The shift position is changed from P to any position other than P.
  • Hazard switch is operated.
  • EPB switch is operated.
  • A brake system malfunction is detected.

(h) Other Points to Note

(1) After entering the deceleration stop phase, the following functions will not be available even if the emergency driving stop system is deactivated. They will available after the BEV system is restarted.

  • Lane Tracing Assist
  • Emergency Driving Stop System*
    • *: Models with Emergency Driving Stop System
