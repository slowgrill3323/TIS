PRE-COLLISION SYSTEM(PCS)

OPERATING CONDITION

(a) Detection of Detectable Objects

(1) Objects are detected based on their size, shape, and movement.

(2) Depending on the ambient brightness, movement, posture and direction of a detectable object, it may not be detected and the system may not operate properly.

(b) Detectable Objects

(1) The system can detect the following as detectable objects. (The target of operation differs depending on the function)

  • Vehicles
  • Pedestrians
  • Bicycles*
  • Motorcycles*
    • *: Detected as a detectable object only when being ridden.
  • Wall

HINT:

Driving support software update <Ver. 02.01>

(c) Operating Conditions of Each Function of the Pre-collision System

(1) The pre-collision system is enabled and the system determines that the possibility of a frontal collision with a detected object is high. However, the system will not operate in the following situations:

  • When the vehicle has not been driven a certain amount after a terminal of the auxiliary battery has been disconnected and reconnected.
  • When the shift position is in R.
  • When the VSC OFF indicator is illuminated (only the pre-collision warning function will be operational).

    HINT:

    For certain vehicle types, the system operates even when the VSC OFF indicator light is illuminated. Refer to the owner's manual for details.

FUNCTION

(a) Pre-collision Warning

(1) When the system determines that the possibility of a collision is high, a buzzer will sound and an icon and warning message will be displayed to urge the driver to take evasive action.

(c) Pre-collision Brake Control

(1) If the system determines that the possibility of a collision is extremely high, the brakes are automatically applied to help avoid the collision or reduce the impact of the collision.

(2) ii. If the vehicle is stopped by pre-collision brake control, it will remain stopped until the brake pedal is depressed. (Except models without EPB or manual transmission)

(d) Suspension Control (Models with Adaptive Variable Suspension system (AVS))

(1) When the system determines that the possibility of a frontal collision is high, the AVS will control the damping force of the shock absorbers to help maintain an appropriate vehicle posture.

(e) Emergency Steering Assist

(1) If the system determines that the following conditions are met, assistance will be provided to help enhance vehicle stability and prevent lane departure.

  • The possibility of a collision is high.
  • There is sufficient space within the lane to perform evasive steering maneuvers.
  • The driver is operating the steering wheel.

(2) During assistance, in addition to the pre-collision warning, the following icon will be displayed on the multi-information display.

(f) Intersection Collision Avoidance Support (Left/Right Turn)

(1) In situations such as the following, if the system determines that the possibility of a collision is high, the pre-collision warning and pre-collision brake control will operate.

  • When turning at an intersection and crossing the path of an oncoming vehicle.
  • When turning left/right and a pedestrian or bicycle is detected.*
  • When turning left/right and an oncoming pedestrian or bicycle is detected.

    HINT:

    *: Driving support software update <Ver. 02.01>

(1) When driving at a low speed, if the accelerator pedal is strongly depressed and the system determines that there is a possibility of a collision, engine output will be restrained or the brakes will be applied weakly to restrict acceleration.
