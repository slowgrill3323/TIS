ACTIVE TRACTION CONTROL (A-TRAC)

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) A-TRAC provides high driving performance when driving on uneven or muddy road surfaces instead of a mechanical differential lock. This system detects the driving condition with various sensors and uses the skid control ECU to control the brake fluid pressure individually for each of the 4 wheels. This suppresses wheel spin and distributes the motive force to provide a powerful Limited Slip Differential (LSD) effect.

Control Overview

(1) A-TRAC Effects

  • Off-road driving performance equivalent to center differential lock and rear differential lock equipment can be obtained.
  • Compared to when the differential lock is applied, the differential functions more as originally designed, without the tight corner braking phenomenon, and with superior line-tracing performance and small-radius turning performance.
  • Vehicle body acceleration caused by the release of the engine brake is suppressed, such as when driving over bumps on a steep downhill slope.

(2) A-TRAC Control Description

  • The vehicle body acceleration condition is detected by the yaw rate sensor, and the tire lock condition and rotation speed of each wheel are detected by the speed sensor. Also, the control target speed for A-TRAC is calculated according to the transfer position.
  • The skid control ECU compares the control target speed and the speed of each wheel, determines whether acceleration slipping is occurring, and performs brake fluid pressure control of each wheel.
