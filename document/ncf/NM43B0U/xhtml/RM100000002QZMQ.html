Title: INTRODUCTION: FEATURES: EQUIPMENT; 2025 MY Tacoma Tacoma HV [12/2024 - 11/2025]

EQUIPMENT

OUTLINE

(a) Stop and Start System

(1) To achieve low emissions and enhanced fuel economy, the stop and start system is provided.

(2) The stop and start system automatically stops the engine when the vehicle comes to a stop, such as when the vehicle stops at a red traffic light. When starting off, the engine is automatically restarted without requiring any extra activity from the driver.

(b) Dynamic Radar Cruise Control System

(1) The dynamic radar cruise control system is provided.

(2) The dynamic radar cruise control system is designed to help reduce driver fatigue by helping to control the vehicle to a speed chosen by the driver. Once the dynamic radar cruise control system is set to the desired vehicle speed, the ECM automatically regulates the motive force to maintain the set vehicle speed without any accelerator pedal operation.

(c) Lane Tracing Assist System

(1) The lane tracing assist system is provided.

(2) The Lane Tracing Assist (LTA) system uses a forward recognition camera installed on the top of the windshield glass to recognize lane markers when driving on highways or controlled-access highways marked with lane markers. The LTA system also uses the forward recognition camera and millimeter wave radar sensor assembly to recognize preceding and surrounding vehicles, as well as to provide information to the driver, performing steering assistance according to road or driving conditions.

(d) Road Sign Assist System

(1) The Road Sign Assist (RSA) system is used. The system recognizes traffic signs using the forward recognition camera and provides information to the driver via the multi-information display and headup display.

(e) Tire Pressure Warning System

(1) A direct-sensing type tire pressure warning system is used.

(2) Since tire pressure decreases over time, regular adjustments are necessary. When the tire pressure warning system detects that the tire inflation pressure has dropped lower than a threshold, it will illuminate the tire pressure warning light to warn the driver.

(g) Active Noise Control System

(1) The active noise control system which reduces vehicle cabin noise and generates engine sounds has been adopted. The active noise control system consists of the Active Noise Control (ANC) and Engine Sound Enhancement (ESE) functions.

(2) The stereo component equalizer assembly controls the active noise control system and generates the sound of ESE function. The stereo component equalizer assembly outputs the generated sound to the audio system speakers.

(h) Telematics System

(1) Telematics system is provided.

(i) Intuitive Parking Assist System

(1) The intuitive parking assist system is provided.

(2) The intuitive parking assist system uses ultrasonic sensors to detect stationary objects in front of, at the corners or at the rear of the vehicle.

(j) Parking Support Brake System

(1) The parking support brake system is provided.

(2) The Parking Support Brake (PKSB) system reduces the risk of damage during accidents by warning the driver, suppressing the propulsion and controlling the brakes when there is a possibility of contact with stationary objects and vehicles approaching from the rear when moving forward or backwards.

(k) Parking Assist Monitor System

(1) The parking assist monitor system is provided.

(2) The parking assist monitor system assists the driver by displaying an image of the view behind the vehicle while backing up, for example while parking. When the display is changed to the wide rear view mode, a wider lateral view behind the vehicle will be displayed.

(l) Blind Spot Monitor System

(1) The blind spot monitor system is provided.

(2) The blind spot monitor system performs 3 functions using the same blind spot monitor sensors: BSM (Blind Spot Monitor) function, RCTA (Rear Cross Traffic Alert) function and Trailer Blind Spot Monitor (TBSM) function.

(3) The BSM function uses sensors to detect vehicles that are in or rapidly approaching the vehicle's blind spot, helping the driver to confirm safety when changing lanes.

(4) If the RCTA function detects a vehicle approaching from the right or left at the rear of the vehicle while the driver is reversing in a parking lot, the function alerts the driver by sounding the RCTA buzzer and flashing the outer rear view mirror indicator light to provide information for the driver, supporting decision making.

(5) The Trailer Blind Spot Monitor (TBSM) function is composed of 3 functions: Trailer Merge Warning (TMW), Auto Trailer Detection (ATD) and Trailer Length Detection (TLD). TMW is a function that expands the BSM detection area based on the length of the trailer. It uses the same means of notifying the driver as BSM. ATD is a function that automatically detects the trailer connection status. TLD is a function that automatically estimates the trailer length and adjusts the BSM detection area.

(m) Multi-terrain Monitor System

(1) The multi-terrain monitor system is provided.

(2) The multi-terrain monitor system is equipped with two functions. The multi-terrain monitor function helps the driver to check for obstacles around the vehicle or tires during off-road driving, by displaying images from under the vehicle and around the vehicle on the multi-display. The panoramic view monitor function helps the driver to check around the vehicle and to drive at low speed or park, by displaying a seamless overhead image (panoramic view) composed of images taken by the high resolution camera.

(n) Panoramic View Monitor System

(1) The panoramic view monitor system is provided.

(2) A panoramic view monitor system is provided which can assist with confirming the conditions around the vehicle, driving at low speeds, and driving during parking by displaying a panoramic view image on the multi-display. The panoramic view image is created by seamlessly combining images from each television camera assembly installed on the front, rear, left and right sides of the vehicle.

(o) Trailer Backup Guide Control

(1) The trailer backup guide control is provided.

(2) The trailer backup guide has 2 modes: "Straight Path Assist (SPA)" for reversing a trailer straight back without the driver having to grip the steering wheel, and "guidance for manual steering backup (Guidance mode)" for providing a trailer angle (jackknife) warning when reversing a trailer straight back and indicating the trailer turning direction on the multi-display.

(p) Garage Door Opener System

(1) The garage door opener system is provided.

(2) The garage door opener system enables a garage door to be opened or closed from inside the vehicle by operating one of the garage door opener switches that is provided in the inner rear view mirror assembly.

(q) Safety Connect System

(1) The safety connect system is provided.

(2) Safety Connect is a service that offers emergency assistance in the event of vehicle theft or an accident that causes the airbag sensor assembly to send an airbag deployment signal, or door lock release signal. In accordance with the situation, the call center quickly calls the police or an ambulance via a Public Safety Answering Point (PSAP) and contacts Roadside Assistance services.

(r) Windshield Deicer System

(1) The windshield deicer system is provided.

(2) The windshield deicer system uses a heater element in the windshield glass to help reduce the formation of ice where the front wipers park.

(s) Power Door Lock Control System

(1) The power door lock control system is provided.

(t) Wireless Door Lock Control System

(1) The wireless door lock control system is provided.

(u) Smart Key System

(1) The smart key system is provided.

(v) Theft Deterrent System

(1) The theft deterrent system is provided.

(2) The theft deterrent system can sound the alarm when somebody attempts to forcibly enter the vehicle, open the engine hood, unlock any door without the electrical key, or when an auxiliary battery terminal is disconnected and reconnected.

(w) Illuminated Entry System

(1) The illuminated entry system is provided.

(2) The illuminated entry system turns the lights on and off in conjunction with the opening and closing or locking and unlocking of the doors, ensuring nighttime usability.

(x) Collision-linked Interior Light Control

(1) The collision-linked interior light control is provided.

(2) Collision-linked interior light control is used to automatically turn the interior lights on when an impact causes the SRS airbags to deploy.

(y) Combination Meter Assembly

(1) A full graphic meter that displays the entire surface with a 12.3-inch color LCD or 2 types of Optitron type combination meter assembly equipped with multi-information display are used.

(z) Headup Display

(1) A headup display is provided.

(2) Various types of information that are necessary when driving are shown on the windshield glass. As a result, the driver can confirm information with less eye movement than when looking at the combination meter.

(aa) Supplemental Restraint System

(1) The dual-stage SRS driver and front passenger airbag supplement the seat belts to help reduce shock to the head and chest of the driver and front passenger in the event of a frontal collision.

(2) The SRS driver and front passenger knee airbag supplement the seat belt to help restrain the occupant's lower body (mainly knees) of the driver and front passenger in the event of a frontal collision, enhancing the effectiveness of occupant protection.

(3) The SRS front side airbags are used to help reduce shock to the chest of the driver and front passenger in the event of a side collision.

(4) The SRS curtain shield airbags are used to help reduce shock to the head of the driver, front passenger and rear outer passengers* in the event of a frontal and side collision.

(5) The Roll Sensing Curtain shield Airbags (RSCA) help to reduce the impact applied to the occupants by operating the seat belt pretensioners for the driver, front passenger and rear outer passengers* and the SRS curtain shield airbag assemblies LH and RH.

HINT:

*: Double cab models

(ab) Occupant Classification System

(1) The occupant classification system is provided.

(2) The occupant classification system judges whether the front passenger seat is occupied by an adult or child (with a child seat) or is unoccupied, in accordance with the load that is applied to the front passenger seat and whether the seat belt is fastened.

(ac) Seat

(1) Depending on the vehicle specifications, an 8-way power seat, a 6-way power + 2-way manual seat or a 6-way manual seat is provided for the driver seat and front passenger seat.

(2) 60/40 Tip-up Cushion and Fold-down Seatback Seat (Double Cab Models)

(3) Depending on the vehicle specifications, an 8-way power seat control system or a 6-way power + 2-way manual seat control system is available for the driver seat and front passenger seat.

(4) A seat heater is available for the driver seat and front passenger seat, depending on the models.

(5) A climate control seat system is available for the driver seat and front passenger seat depending on the models.

(6) Fabric or synthetic leather is provided for the seat cover, depending on the models.

(ad) Air Conditioning System

(1) A dual zone (left and right independent temperature control) automatic air conditioning system is provided depending on the model.

(ae) Auxiliary Switch System

(1) The auxiliary switch system is provided.

(2) There are auxiliary switches located on the left side of the instrument panel that control 12 V DC fused accessory circuits throughout the vehicle to which electrical accessories can be connected.

(af) Rear Window Defogger System

(1) The rear window defogger system is provided.

(2) The rear window defogger system uses the heater wires on the back window glass to defog the back window glass.

(ag) Wireless Charging System

(1) A wireless charging system has been provided next to the shift lever.

(2) The wireless charging system allows Qi-compatible* devices, such as cellular telephones and smartphones, to be charged without the use of a charging cable by placing them on the charging area of the mobile wireless charger cradle assembly.

HINT:

*: The Qi standard is an international wireless charging standard set by the Wireless Power Consortium (WPC). This system supports Qi standard Ver. 1.2.4 and allows a power output within the range specified by Power Class 0.

"Qi" and the "Qi" logo are registered trademarks of the Wireless Power Consortium (WPC).

(ah) Power Outlet Socket System (Interior)

(1) A Dual USB-C power outlet socket has been provided on the instrument panel upper tray. Additionally, a Dual USB-C power outlet socket has been provided on the console rear end panel sub-assembly, depending on the model.

(2) A DC 12 V power outlet socket has been provided on the instrument panel upper tray and rear end panel sub-assembly, depending on the model.

(3) An AC 120 V power outlet socket has been provided on the console rear end panel, depending on the model.

(4) The main switch that enables the accessory power outlet system (120 VAC) to be turned ON/OFF, is located on the driver side instrument panel. Also, the power outlet switch has an indicator light in consideration of usability.

(ai) Sliding Roof System

(1) The sliding roof system uses a sliding roof drive gear sub-assembly to perform the tilt up-and-down and open-and-close operations.

(aj) Power Tail Gate System

(1) The power tail gate system enables the tail gate to be opened and closed automatically by a motor in accordance with signals from the power tail gate control switch (tail gate opener switch), tail gate handle switch (tail gate opener switch assembly), elbow switch (luggage electrical key switch) or power tail gate button on an electrical key transmitter sub-assembly.

(ak) Fuel Lid Opener System (Models with Fuel Lid Opener System)

(1) An electric type fuel lid opener system is used.

(al) Wiper and Washer System

(1) The wiper motor is directly mounted in the center of the wiper link, reducing wiper operating sounds and decreasing fluctuations in the wiping range.

(am) Light Emitting Diode (LED) Headlight System

(1) The LED headlight system use LEDs as its light source. This system consists of LEDs and headlight computer.

(an) Daytime Running Light System

(1) The daytime running light system is provided.

(2) The daytime running light system is designed to automatically illuminate the Light Emitting Diode (LED) lights during the daytime to keep the vehicle highly visible to other vehicles.

(ao) Light Auto Turn-off System

(1) The light auto turn-off system is provided.

(2) The light auto turn-off system is used to prevent the driver from leaving the vehicle with the headlights, parking lights, front side marker lights, front fog lights*, taillights, rear side marker lights, or license plate lights on.

(ap) Manual Headlight Beam Level Control System

(1) The manual headlight beam level control system is provided.

(2) The manual headlight beam level control system keeps the beam of the headlight adjusted to the appropriate level in accordance with the number (weight) of passengers and volume of luggage.

(aq) Automatic Light Control System

(1) The automatic light control system is provided.

(2) When the light control switch is in the AUTO position, the automatic light control sensor detects the ambient light level and automatically turns the headlights, parking lights, front side marker lights, taillights, rear side marker lights, and license plate lights on or off accordingly.

(ar) Welcome Light Illumination System

(1) The welcome light illumination system is provided.

(2) The welcome light illumination system operates the parking lights, front side marker lights, taillights, rear side marker lights, and license plate lights in conjunction with the locking and unlocking of the doors, ensuring nighttime usability.

(as) Headlight On/Off Reminder Control System

(1) The headlight on/off reminder control system is provided.

(2) When the user forgets to turn the headlights on or off, a warning message is displayed on the multi-information display in the combination meter assembly.

(at) Wiper-linked Low Beam Headlight Control

(1) The wiper-linked low beam headlight control is provided.

(2) Wiper-linked low beam headlight control is designed to automatically illuminate the low beam headlights when it is raining to make the vehicle more visible to other vehicles.

(au) Collision-linked Control System

(1) The collision-linked control system is provided.

(2) When a collision occurs where the SRS airbags are deployed, the turn signal lights are flashed to inform people near the vehicle of its location.

(av) Sequential Turn Signal Lights

(1) The sequential turn signal lights is provided.

(2) The turn signal lights illuminate in sequence, intuitively conveying the driver's intention to change course.

(aw) Lane Change Flasher System

(1) The lane change flasher system is provided.

(2) The lane change flasher system flashes the turn signal light a certain number of times when the turn signal switch is moved to the lane change position. By using the lane change flasher system when changing lanes, it is no longer necessary to hold the turn signal switch in the lane change position.

(ax) Cargo Compartment Light Control

(1) The cargo compartment light control is provided.

(2) Cargo lights and bed lights* are provided to enhance usability. They are designed to light up the rear deck of the vehicle and should only be operated during cargo loading/unloading.

  • *: Models with bed light

(ay) Trailer Hitch Light Control (Models with Trailer Hitch Light)

(1) The trailer hitch light control is provided.

(2) An additional light on the tailgate of the truck which will provide the vehicle with the lighting needed when the trailer backup guide feature is being activated.* The trailer hitch light will also turn on when in reverse to assist with vision and when the cargo lights are on.

  • *: Models with trailer backup guide

(az) LED Illumination System (Models with Center LED Illumination Light)

(1) Center LED illumination light that can be turned on and off using a switch when the high beam headlights are illuminated is provided.

(ba) Toyota Safety Sense

(1) The Toyota Safety Sense consists of the driving assist systems and contributes to a safe and comfortable driving experience:

  • PCS (Pre-Collision System)
  • Dynamic Radar Cruise Control (DRCC)
  • LTA (Lane Tracing Assist)
  • LDA (Lane Departure Alert)
  • EDSS (Emergency Driving Stop System)*1
  • RSA (Road Sign Assist)*2
  • AHB (Automatic High Beam System)
  • PDA (Proactive Driving Assist)*3
    • *1: Models with EDSS (Emergency Driving Stop System)
    • *2: Models with RSA (Road Sign Assist)
    • *3: Models with PDA (Proactive Driving Assist)

(bb) Pre-collision System (PCS)

(1) The pre-collision system is provided.

(2) The pre-collision system uses sensors to detect objects in the path of the vehicle. When the system determines that the possibility of a frontal collision with a detected object is high, a warning operates to urge the driver to take evasive action and the potential brake pressure is increased to help the driver avoid the collision. If the system determines that the possibility of a collision is extremely high, the brakes are automatically applied to help avoid the collision or help reduce the impact of the collision.
