CHARGING CONTROL

FUNCTION OF MAIN COMPONENTS

Component

Function

Hybrid Vehicle Control ECU
  • Detects the electrical load caused by certain electrical components.
  • Calculates the required voltage based on the state of the auxiliary battery detected by the battery state sensor assembly and sends a signal indicating the required voltage to the hybrid motor control inverter assembly (DC/DC converter).
  • Sends a generator assembly operation request signal to the ECM when the discharge amount of the hybrid motor control inverter assembly (DC/DC converter) and the discharge amount of the auxiliary battery detected by the battery state sensor assembly exceed a certain amount.

ECM

Receives the generator assembly operation request signal from the hybrid vehicle control ECU and sends a drive signal to the generator assembly.

Hybrid Motor Control Inverter Assembly (DC/DC Converter)
  • Receives the target voltage command from the hybrid vehicle control ECU and controls the output voltage.
  • Supplies power to each ECU and auxiliary equipment.

Generator Assembly

Battery State Sensor Assembly

Detects the charge/discharge current, voltage, temperature and self-diagnosis result of the auxiliary battery and sends signals to the hybrid vehicle control ECU.

Auxiliary Battery

Supplies power to each ECU and auxiliary equipment.

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) When the power consumption has exceeded the output power of the hybrid motor control inverter assembly (DC/DC converter), after a certain amount is discharged from the auxiliary battery, the engine starts and the generator assembly starts to generate power.

(b) When power generation is unnecessary in accordance with the electrical load conditions, the generator assembly is stopped.

DIAGNOSIS

(a) The system detects a malfunction, ECM or hybrid vehicle control ECU records information related to the fault. Furthermore, the Malfunction Indicator Lamp (MIL) in the combination meter assembly illuminates or blinks or displaying information on the multi-information display to inform the driver.

(b) The ECM or hybrid vehicle control ECU also stores Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) for malfunctions it has detected.

(c) The DTCs can be accessed by using the GTS.

(d) For details, refer to the Repair Manual.
