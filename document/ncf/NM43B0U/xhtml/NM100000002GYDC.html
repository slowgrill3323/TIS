TIMING CHAIN AND CHAIN TENSIONER

CONSTRUCTION

(a) A bushing chain with a 8.0 mm (0.315 in.) pitch is used.

(b) A resin material is used for the chain sliding part of the No. 1 chain vibration damper and No. 2 chain vibration damper and has a friction reduction effect with high abrasion resistance. Furthermore, resin is used for the chain tensioner slipper resulting in reduced weight.

(c) The No. 1 chain tensioner assembly uses a spring and oil pressure to maintain proper chain tension at all times. The No. 1 chain tensioner assembly suppresses noise generated by the chain sub-assembly.

(d) By changing the layout of the high pressure chamber and low pressure chamber, the high pressure chamber holds oil even if left for a long time, resulting in increased quietness.

(e) By using aluminum for the tensioner body, weight reduction is achieved and serviceability has been enhanced by discontinuing the ratchet mechanism.
