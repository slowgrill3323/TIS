SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) Early Stage Injection Control

(1) Early stage injection control shortens the time it takes the engine to restart after the stop and start system stops the engine, thus allowing smooth initial acceleration.

(2) The ECM memorizes the crankshaft angle detected by the crank position sensor when the engine is stopped by the stop and start system.

(3) The ECM judges the injection required and decides which cylinder to ignite when the driver does restart operation. The ECM then uses this information as it starts the engine.

(b) Hill-start Assist Function

(1) The driving force (creep phenomenon) of a former AT vehicle is regulated by depressing the brake pedal while stopped, and the driving force is transmitted by releasing the brake pedal when starting the vehicle. However, when starting a stop and start system equipped vehicle with its engine stopped due to the activation of the idle stop function, there is a short time delay before the driving force is generated as the engine will start after the brake pedal is released.

(2) The hill-start assist function is a system to assist starting of a stop and start system equipped vehicle on a slope to deal with the situation described above, retaining the brake hydraulic pressure until sufficient driving force is generated.

HINT:

  • The hill-start assist function operates not only on a slope but also on a level surface.
  • Abnormal sounds may be heard, but this does not mean the brake is malfunctioning.
  • The brake pedal response may change or it may vibrate, but this does not mean it is malfunctioning.

(c) System Prohibit Control

(1) For safety, battery protection, comfort and ECU learning reasons, the engine stop and start ECU prohibits stop and start system operation if any one of the following conditions is met.

Prohibition Reason

Condition
*: If the value learned by the ECU has been cleared by disconnecting the battery or for another reason, stop and start system operation will be prohibited until ECU learning is complete.

Safety

If the engine is started with the hood open, such as when jump starting, engine restart operation cannot be ensured. Therefore, stop and start system operation will be prohibited. The operation of the system will be restored on the next trip.

If the driver door or hood is opened before the engine stops, stop and start system operation will be prohibited for safety reasons.

The brake booster vacuum is insufficient.

The driver seat belt is unfastened before the engine stops.

The engine catalyst is being quickly warmed up.

Battery Protection

The refresh charge continues for 0.5 to 1 hour, and is carried out approximately every 20 hours of driving. While refresh charge, stop and start system operation will be disabled.

When the idling stop rate becomes higher than the predetermined rate, stop and start system operation will be disabled.

When the battery voltage becomes low and the battery integrated current value drops to below the threshold value, stop and start operation will be disabled.

Comfort

If the air conditioning is on when the outside temperature is high and the evaporator temperature is high, stop and start system operation will be prohibited.

If the air conditioning is on when the outside temperature is low, stop and start system operation will be prohibited.

If the heater is on when the outside temperature is low and the engine coolant temperature is low, stop and start system operation will be prohibited.

If the front DEF switch and blower switch are on, stop and start system operation will be prohibited.

ECU Learning

ECU learning is not complete.*

(d) Warning Control

(1) While the stop and start system stops the engine, one of following operations warns the driver: sounding the multi-buzzer in combination meter assembly, blinking the stop and start indicator light, displaying the multi-information display in combination meter assembly, or restarting the engine or stopping it from restarting.

Item

Operating Condition
*1: When the battery fluid temperature becomes -15°C (5°F) or lower or 65°C (149°F) or higher, control is prohibited. After control is prohibited and the battery fluid temperature rises to -10°C (14°F) or higher or falls to 60°C (140°F) or lower, 55°C (131°F) or lower, control is performed.

*2: Regarding Battery Integrated Current

In the stop and start system, the engine stop and start ECU switches the system control mode (stop and start system control permitted/prohibited) based on the battery condition (charge/discharge condition) to protect the battery and to ensure stable engine restarting performance.

The battery charge/discharge condition is determined from the integrated current value calculated from the battery state sensor assembly signal. The integrated current value is obtained by multiplying the current (ampere) detected by the battery state sensor assembly by the time (seconds), and is expressed in the unit A-sec. This can be measured by the GTS.

The engine stop and start ECU determines the power charge based on the integrated current value and prohibits stop and start system control if the value becomes the threshold or less, because the battery might not be able to start the engine. The threshold varies according to the battery temperature and status of battery charge control.

*3: After the integrated current value becomes -5544 A-sec or less, in order to charge the battery, control is prohibited until the value becomes -5299 A-sec or more.

*4: After the integrated current value becomes -990 A-sec or less, in order to charge the battery, control is prohibited until the value becomes 0 A-sec or more.

*5: When the engine is restarted, ensure proper braking force so that the vehicle does not advance.

(f) Idling Stop Control during Brake Hold Function Operation

(1) While the brake hold function is operating during the idle stop condition, the idle stop condition continues even if the brake pedal is released.

(2) By depressing the accelerator pedal, the engine restarts and the brake hold function is canceled.

HINT:

  • Depending on the vehicle condition, idle stop may not be performed even during brake hold.
  • Depending on the vehicle condition, the engine may restart during brake hold. In this case, the vehicle will not start off since brake hold continues.
  • If any of the operation conditions are not met during engine stop by the stop and start system, the brake hold system will not operate. If the electrical parking brake is applied automatically, the engine will restart.

(g) Idle stop control during dynamic radar cruise control

(1) If the vehicle stops through the stop control function while the dynamic radar cruise control is operating, idle stop is performed together with brake hold function operation.

(2) If a vehicle ahead accelerating is detected, the engine automatically restarts.*

HINT:

*: Even if the engine restarts, brake hold continues. Therefore, the vehicle does not automatically start off.

HINT:

  • Depending on the vehicle condition, idle stop may not be performed even during brake hold.
  • Depending on the vehicle condition, the engine may restart during brake hold. In this case, the vehicle will not start off since brake hold continues.
  • If a certain time has elapsed after the vehicle was stopped in vehicle-to-vehicle distance control mode and the electric parking brake is applied automatically, the engine will restart.

FUNCTION

(a) Settings of Stop and Start System

(1) The engine stop operation time due to stop and start control when the air conditioning system is on can be changed between Standard and Extended (the stop and start system has priority).

DIAGNOSIS

(a) When the engine stop and start ECU detects a malfunction and CAN communication is normal, the engine stop and start ECU records information related to the malfunction. Furthermore, the stop and start cancel indicator light in the combination meter assembly blinks to inform the driver that CAN communication is normal.

(b) The engine stop and start ECU also stores Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) related to malfunctions. DTCs can be read using the GTS.
