DIFFERENTIAL LOCK CONTROL

FUNCTION OF MAIN COMPONENTS

Component

Function

Rear Differential

Electronic Differential Lock Case Sub-assembly

Switches the rear differential between free mode and lock mode in accordance with signals from the 4 wheel drive control ECU.

Differential Lock Indicator Switch

Detects the rear differential position (free mode or lock mode).

Transfer Shift Actuator Assembly

4WD Detection Switch

Detects the transfer shift position (2WD or 4H).

4L Detection Switch

Detects the transfer shift position (4L).

A.D.D. Shift Actuator

A.D.D. Position Switch

Detects the actuator shift position (lock or free).

Combination Switch Assembly

Rear Differential Lock Switch

Selects the rear differential position (free mode or lock mode).

4 Wheel Drive Control ECU

Controls the coil assembly in the electronic differential lock case sub-assembly based on the signals from each switch and the vehicle speed signal from the skid control ECU.

Brake Actuator Assembly

Skid Control ECU
  • Sends the vehicle speed signal to the 4 wheel drive control ECU.
  • Sends the brake control operation condition signals to the 4 wheel drive control ECU.

Combination Meter Assembly

Rear Differential Lock Indicator

Illuminates or flashes to inform the driver of the rear differential lock system condition.

ABS Warning Indicator

Turns on in accordance with the rear differential lock system mode when ABS function is not operating.

Multi-information Display

Displays a message in accordance with the rear differential lock system mode when TRAC function is not operating.

Buzzer

Sounds when the switching conditions are not met while switching the rear differential lock system mode.

SYSTEM CONTROL

(a) When all of the following conditions are met, the rear differential lock operates.

  • Transfer limit switch and transfer indicator switch detect 4L.*1
  • The vehicle speed is 8 km/h (5 mph) or lower.*2
  • The rear differential lock switch is pushed.

HINT:

*1: 4WD models

*2: The rear differential should not be switched from free mode to lock mode while the vehicle is turning or while the wheels are spinning.

(b) The 4 wheel drive control ECU uses the buzzer and rear differential lock indicator in the combination meter assembly to display the condition of the rear differential lock system.

DIAGNOSIS

(a) When the 4 wheel drive control ECU detects a malfunction, the 4 wheel drive control ECU records the malfunction and memorizes the information related to the fault. Furthermore, the 4 wheel drive control ECU flashes the rear differential lock indicator in the combination meter to inform the driver.

(b) The 4 wheel drive control ECU will also store the Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) of the malfunctions. The DTCs stored in the 4 wheel drive control ECU are output to the GTS via the DLC3.

(c) For details, refer to the Repair Manual.
