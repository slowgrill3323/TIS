CAMSHAFT

CONSTRUCTION

(a) The cam profile has been designed with an indented R (radius). This results in increased valve lift when the valve begins to open and finishes closing, helping to achieve enhanced output performance.

(b) A timing rotor for the camshaft position sensor is provided on the intake camshaft and exhaust camshaft.

(c) The cam that drives the fuel (engine room side) pump assembly (for high pressure side) is positioned between the No. 4 cylinder and the end of the exhaust camshaft to achieve size reduction. Also, a pump cam with 4 peaks is used in the cam that drives the fuel (engine room side) pump assembly (for high pressure side) and by synchronizing fuel pressure-feeding and fuel injection, the difference in fuel pressure between cylinders is reduced.

(d) The vacuum pump assembly is driven by the end of the exhaust camshaft.
