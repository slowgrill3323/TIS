SAFETY FEATURES

FUNCTION

(a) Impact Absorbing Structure for Frontal Collision

(1) In order to enhance passenger protection performance during frontal collisions, a vehicle structure that optimally absorbs and disperses the impact load has been developed.

  • During a frontal collision, the collision load from the upper member is distributed to the rail, door reinforcement, and rocker via the front body pillar.

(b) Impact Absorbing Structure for Side Collision

(1) Hot-stamped steel, ultra high-tensile strength steel, and high tensile strength steel are used for the body frame to increase strength and suppress deformation of the cabin.

  • The center pillar inner reinforcement, center pillar outer reinforcement, front pillar inner reinforcement and front pillar outer reinforcement also use hot-stamped steel. In addition, the center pillar side member integrates the upper and lower to reduce deformation.
  • Ultra high tensile strength steel is used for the roof panel reinforcement center, front header, rocker outer, and rocker inner to reduce cabin deformation.

(c) Reduction of Pedestrian Head Injury

(1) A deformation stroke is ensured for the front part of the engine hood to absorb impact during collisions, creating a structure that reduces the impact to the head of a pedestrian during collisions.
