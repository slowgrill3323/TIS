Title: L580F (HYBRID TRANSMISSION / TRANSAXLE): HYBRID TRANSMISSION SYSTEM: GENERAL; 2024 - 2026 MY Tacoma Tacoma HV [03/2024 -        ]

GENERAL

OUTLINE

  • A L580F hybrid vehicle transmission is used.
  • A lightweight, compact and highly efficient 8-speed gear train is used.
  • A wide range of gear ratios is used, dramatically improving start-off acceleration performance by using a low gear ratio for the 1st gear and fuel economy at high speeds by using high gear for the middle and high gears. Also, a close gear ratio is used for the low gear positions to achieve rhythmical gear changes. Furthermore, the transmission constantly selects the optimal gear ratio to achieve improved fuel economy in situations such as city driving.
  • The gear train is composed of 2 planetary gears.
  • An oil level detection mechanism (overflow method) is used. For details of fluid filling and adjustment procedures, refer to the Repair Manual.
  • Due to the use of hybrid system control, an oil pump with motor assembly is installed inside the automatic transmission assembly. When the engine is restarted, the oil pressure required for the clutch and brake engagement is supplied to make smooth starting off possible.
  • A hybrid module assembly is set between the engine and transmission. As a result, high torque output and improved fuel economy are achieved.
  • The hybrid module assembly is composed of a Motor Generator (MG) that is used as the motor, a valve body with 2 solenoid valves, and 1 clutch.

MAIN FEATURES

(a) Aim of Development

  1. A direct driving feel
  2. Rhythmical shifting
  3. Superior fuel economy performance
  4. A top-class level of compactness
  5. Achieving both quietness when driving at high speed and off-road driveability

(b) Close Gear Ratios and Wide Gear Ratios

  • Close gear ratios are used to achieve rhythmical shifting with a good tempo during acceleration.
  • Wide gear ratios are used to achieve smooth and powerful start-off acceleration performance in low gears and generate more marginal driving force in high gears, achieving powerful and linear lunching performance.
