INVERTER WITH CONVERTER

CONSTRUCTION

(a) Hybrid Motor Control Inverter Assembly

(1) The hybrid motor control inverter assembly has a multi-layer structure which consists of the Motor Generator ECU (MG ECU), inverter, condenser and DC/DC converter, achieving a compact design.

(2) The hybrid motor control inverter assembly is cooled by the dedicated radiator of a cooling system that is separate from that of the engine cooling system.

(3) In consideration of safety, interlock switches are provided for the hybrid motor control inverter assembly. The interlock switches turn off the SMRs when the upper inverter cover sub-assembly is removed or the connector of the power cable (floor under wire) is disconnected.

(b) Motor Generator ECU (MG ECU)

(1) The MG ECU is provided in the hybrid motor control inverter assembly. In accordance with signals received from the ECM, the MG ECU controls the inverter in order to drive the MG or to cause them to generate electricity.

(2) The MG ECU transmits information that is required for vehicle control, such as the inverter output amperage, inverter temperature and failure information, to the hybrid vehicle control ECU assembly. The MG ECU receives information that is required for controlling the MG, such as the required motive force or the motor temperature, from the hybrid vehicle control ECU assembly and ECM.

(c) Inverter Current Sensor

(1) The inverter current sensors detect the amperage of the three-phase alternating current that actuates the MG. This actual amperage is used as feedback by the MG ECU.

(2) Current sensors are used for the current sent to the three-phase windings of the MG. The current sensors are located in the hybrid motor control inverter assembly for the U, V and W phases of the MG.

(d) Inverter Temperature Sensor

(1) In the hybrid motor control inverter assembly, there are 7 different temperature sensors: 1 sensor for the coolant temperature and 1 sensor for the each IGBTs.

(2) The MG ECU confirms the effectiveness of the inverter cooling system based on the temperature information sent from these sensors. In addition, inverter output is limited when the temperature is high.
