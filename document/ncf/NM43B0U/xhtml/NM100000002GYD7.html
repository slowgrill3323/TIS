CYLINDER BLOCK

CONSTRUCTION

(a) An aluminum cylinder block sub-assembly is used to achieve a lightweight design.

(b) The rigidity around the crank journal has been increased by combining the crankshaft bearing cap and bearing cap, suitable for high speeds and high power output.

(c) By using a cylinder liner (cast iron) with a spiny-type outer surface, adhesion to the cylinder bore (aluminum) is increased and heat dissipation is improved, reducing piston temperature and suppressing cylinder bore deformation.

(d) Cooling passages are drilled between each cylinder bore to provide increased cooling to the upper part of the cylinder bore which is most susceptible to heat. This minimizes deformation of the cylinder bore, ensuring high power output and reduced oil consumption.

(e) A larger breather hole is provided in the No. 1 to No. 4 journal wall. As a result, friction is reduced and output performance is improved.

(f) The angle of the bore cross hatching, the polishing on the cylinder liner surface, is at an angle of 30°, thus improving the oil retention of the interior of the cylinder bore. This reduces the friction between the cylinder bore and the piston, improving fuel consumption.

(g) The shape of the ribs, and the positioning and shape of the knock control sensor have been optimized, thus improving control of knocking.

Thus, the side force to the cylinder wall is reduced when the maximum pressure is applied. This contributes to fuel economy.
