Title: BRAKE CONTROL / DYNAMIC CONTROL SYSTEMS: BRAKE CONTROL SYSTEM (for Gasoline Model): GENERAL; 2024 - 2026 MY Tacoma [12/2023 -        ]

GENERAL

OUTLINE

(a) The following brake control functions are provided: Anti-lock Brake System (ABS), Electronic Brake force Distribution (EBD), brake assist, Traction Control (TRAC), Active Traction Control (A-TRAC)*1, Vehicle Stability Control (VSC), steering cooperative control, trailer sway control, Auto limited slip differential (AUTO LSD)*2, hill-start assist control, downhill assist control*3, CRAWL*3 and brake hold.

  • *1: 4WD models
  • *2: 2WD models
  • *3: Models with DAC/CRAWL and MTS switch

(b) The skid control ECU processes signals from sensors and performs brake control.

(c) A secondary collision brake system is used. This system lessens damage from a secondary collision by controlling the automatic brakes and stop light illumination after a collision occurs.

(d) Multi-terrain select is used. When the driver performs a switch operation to select an appropriate mode for the road conditions, vehicle control switches to the optimal control for the road conditions. As a result, optimal driving performance is maintained, achieving superior off-road driveability.*

  • *: Models with DAC/CRAWL and MTS switch

PRECAUTION

(a) ABS

(1) The ABS system cannot increase the performance limit of the tires. Always drive safely and be aware of the vehicle speed and distance to other vehicles.

(2) Any non-standard components such as non-standard tires that are installed may have a negative effect on the ABS function.

(3) When braking using ABS, the braking distance may be longer than models without ABS in the following conditions:

  • When driving on a dirt road or a road covered with fresh snow
  • When using tire chains
  • When driving over a joint in the road surface
  • When driving on a rough road such as an unpaved or stone-paved road

(b) Brake Assist

(1) Brake assist cannot increase the performance limit of the fundamental brake system. Always drive safely and be aware of the vehicle speed and distance to other vehicles.

(c) TRAC, VSC

(1) The TRAC and VSC systems ensure traction and vehicle stability. Do not disable the TRAC and VSC functions unless necessary.

(2) The TRAC and VSC systems cannot increase the performance limit of the tires. Always drive the vehicle carefully in conditions in which drive wheel traction or vehicle stability may be lost.

(d) Trailer Sway Control

(1) Trailer sway control is a part of the VSC function. Accordingly, if the VSC OFF mode or VSC is not functioning normally, trailer sway control will not operate.

(2) Trailer sway control will be unable to stabilize the vehicle if a trailer is being towed which cannot be physically controlled due to the influence of the vehicle, road surface, driving conditions, etc.

(e) Hill-start Assist Control

(1) Hill-start assist control is not intended to keep the vehicle stationary on a hill for a prolonged period. As hill-start assist control only prevents the vehicle from rolling backwards for 2 seconds after the brake pedal is released, perform start-off within this period.

(2) When hill-start assist control is operating and the ignition switch is turned off, hill-start assist control will be canceled without warning.

(3) Hill-start assist control prevents the vehicle from rolling backwards by controlling the brake force to the 4 wheels. However, the vehicle may not remain stationary when on an extremely steep slope or a low friction surface such as ice.

(f) Brake Hold

(1) The brake hold is not for parking the vehicle for a long period of time in the same way as the parking brake.

(2) If brake hold control does not operate even when the vehicle stops, depress the brake pedal again firmly to stop the vehicle. If the vehicle still does not stop, confirm the conditions for brake hold control to operate.

(3) When the ignition switch is turned off during brake hold operation, control stops.

(4) This function may not stop the vehicle on steep slopes and slippery roads.

(g) Multi-terrain Select

(1) Check that the multi-terrain select indicator light is illuminated before driving.

(2) Multi-terrain select is not intended to expand the performance limits of the vehicle. Thoroughly check the road conditions and driving route in advance, and drive with caution.

(3) The information about each mode that can be selected in multi-terrain select is for reference only. The mode may not be effective enough depending on the road conditions (pitch, road surface friction coefficient and undulations). Thoroughly check the road conditions before driving.

(4) Multi-terrain select controls the vehicle so that the motive force is maximized to improve off-road driveability. Therefore, the fuel economy performance may not be as good as when driving without multi-terrain select.

(5) Only use multi-terrain select during off-road driving.

(6) Using multi-terrain select continuously for a long time and applying a high load to its related parts may prevent sufficiently effective operation. Also, when the brake temperature rises too much, the brake control of multi-terrain select stops temporarily, and the buzzer sounds continuously and TRAC OFF is displayed on the multi-information display to inform the driver. In such cases, stop the vehicle immediately in a safe location and allow the brake related parts to cool sufficiently. When the temperature drops after waiting a while, the brake control of multi-terrain select becomes available for operation again.

(7) When multi-terrain select operates, vibration may be felt from the vehicle body or steering wheel, or an operating sound may be heard from inside the engine room. This is not a malfunction.

(h) Downhill Assist Control

(1) Check that the downhill assist control indicator light is illuminated before driving.

(2) While downhill assist control is operating, the slip indicator light blinks and the brake lights illuminate.

(3) When driving down a steep hill that is likely to make downhill assist control operate, use the engine brake as well while driving downhill. This enables stable driving of the vehicle and reduces the load on the brake.

(4) Using downhill assist control continuously for a long time and applying a high load to its related parts may prevent sufficiently effective operation. Also, when the brake temperature rises too much, downhill assist control stops temporarily, and the buzzer sounds continuously and the downhill assist control indicator light blinks to inform the driver. In such cases, stop the vehicle immediately in a safe location and allow the brake related parts to cool sufficiently. When the temperature drops after waiting a while, downhill assist control becomes available for operation again.

(5) On extremely steep slopes and extremely slippery surfaces such as snow-covered roads, it may not be possible to maintain a set vehicle speed.

(i) CRAWL

(1) Check that the CRAWL indicator light is illuminated before driving.

(2) CRAWL is not intended to expand the performance limits of the vehicle. Thoroughly check the road conditions and driving route in advance, and drive with caution.

(3) When CRAWL control ends for a reason other than a CRAWL switch operation, the CRAWL indicator changes from illuminated to blinking and the buzzer sounds intermittently. In such cases, take appropriate measures, such as stopping the vehicle.

(4) Using CRAWL continuously for a long time and applying a high load to its related parts may prevent sufficiently effective operation. Also, when the brake temperature rises too much, CRAWL stops temporarily, and the buzzer sounds continuously and the CRAWL indicator light blinks and then turns off to inform the driver. In such cases, stop the vehicle immediately in a safe location and allow the brake related parts to cool sufficiently. When the temperature drops after waiting a while, CRAWL becomes available for operation again.

(5) Using CRAWL continuously for long time may cause the oil temperature of the automatic transmission to rise too much. Continuing to drive under such conditions will cause an oil leak from the automatic transmission or a malfunction. In such cases, stop the vehicle immediately in a safe location and allow the automatic transmission to cool sufficiently.

(6) On extremely steep slopes, very rough surfaces such as rocky roads, and extremely slippery surfaces such as snow-covered roads, it may not be possible to maintain a set vehicle speed.

(j) AUTO LSD

(1) When operating the VSC OFF switch, ensure that the vehicle wheels are not spinning freely.

(2) When AUTO LSD is available for operation, the AUTO LSD indicator light illuminates.

(3) While AUTO LSD is operating, the slip indicator light in the combination meter blinks.

(4) When the AUTO LSD control is continuously operated for a long time, the temperature of the brake actuator increases. If the temperature increases too much, the AUTO LSD indicator light will blink to notify the driver. AUTO LSD will then be temporarily stopped to protect the system. AUTO LSD will become available for operation again once the AUTO LSD indicator light turns off after a short while. The vehicle can be driven normally while this occurs.

(5) The AUTO LSD applies brakes to the driving wheel tires and controls the brake pressure, which produces the LSD effect. However, start-off performance cannot be ensured on steep slopes or road surfaces with extremely low road surface resistance such as snow-covered roads.
