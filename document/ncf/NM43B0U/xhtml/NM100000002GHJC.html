POWER TAIL GATE LOCK

CONSTRUCTION

(a) Tail Gate Lock Assembly

(1) The tail gate lock assembly consists of 4 position detection switches (initial switch, pawl switch, latch switch and tail gate courtesy switch), tail gate lock motor, active lever, passive lever, bell crank lever, open lever, latch sub-assembly, latch lever and latch.

(2) When this system does not operate due to a discharged auxiliary battery or other malfunctions, the power tail gate can be opened by directly operating the open lever in the tail gate lock assembly.

(d) Latch Switch and Tail Gate Courtesy Switch

(1) The latch switch and tail gate courtesy switch turn on when the power tail gate is open.

(2) The latch switch turns off and the tail gate courtesy switch turns on when the power tail gate is ajar (half-latch).

(3) The latch switch and tail gate courtesy switch turn off when the power tail gate is fully closed (full-latch).

OPERATION

(a) Release Operation

(1) The tail gate lock motor rotates in the release direction with each gear in the initial position, transmitting rotation to the pinion.

(2) The pinion operates the active lever, bell crank lever, open lever, latch lever, and latch sub-assembly, in that order.

(3) The open lever operates to release the latch via the latch sub-assembly, releasing the striker.

(b) Fully Close Operation

(1) When the power tail gate becomes ajar, each built-in detection switch detects the ajar position and the tail gate lock motor performs an engagement operation to fully close the tail gate.
