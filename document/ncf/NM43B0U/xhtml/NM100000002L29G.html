ACOUSTIC VEHICLE ALERTING CONTROL

FUNCTION OF MAIN COMPONENTS

Component

Function

Vehicle Approaching Speaker Controller

Controls starting and stopping of operation, sound pitch and volume level based on signals received from hybrid vehicle control ECU assembly, ECM and No. 2 skid control ECU.

Front Vehicle Approaching Speaker Assembly (Vehicle Approaching Speaker Assembly)

Emits warning sounds based on the operation signal received from the vehicle approaching speaker controller.

Rear Vehicle Approaching Speaker Assembly (No. 2 Vehicle Approaching Speaker Assembly)

Combination Meter Assembly

  • Multi-information Display

Displays a warning message when there is a malfunction in the acoustic vehicle alerting system.

Hybrid Vehicle Control ECU Assembly
  • Outputs a shift position signal to the vehicle approaching speaker controller.
  • Outputs an engine running signal to the vehicle approaching speaker controller.

ECM

Outputs a stop light switch signal to the vehicle approaching speaker controller.

Brake Actuator Assembly

  • No. 2 Skid Control ECU

Outputs a vehicle speed signal to the vehicle approaching speaker controller.

Central Gateway ECU (Network Gateway ECU)

Transmits the signal between the CAN communication bus.

OPERATING CONDITION

Condition

Outline

System Active

The acoustic vehicle alerting system operates (emits a warning sound) when all of the following conditions are met:

  • The shift state is other than park (P).
  • The engine is stopped.
  • The vehicle speed is 35 km/h (22 mph) or less.

System Stop

The acoustic vehicle alerting system stops operating when any of the following conditions is met:

  • The shift state is park (P).
  • The engine starts.
  • The vehicle speed is more than 35 km/h (22 mph).

HINT:

All conditions are the same when the vehicle reverses.

SYSTEM CONTROL

Control

Outline

Vehicle Speed Linked Control
  • Emits the warning sound by controlling the pitch of the sound in accordance with the vehicle speed.
  • The warning sound becomes higher as the vehicle accelerates and becomes lower as the vehicle decelerates.

Fade-in/Fade Out Control

When the system starts operating, the warning sound volume gradually increases, and when system operation is stopped, the sound volume gradually decreases.
