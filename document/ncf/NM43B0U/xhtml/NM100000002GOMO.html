FUEL INJECTOR (FOR DIRECT INJECTION)

CONSTRUCTION

(a) High-pressure, 6-hole type direct fuel injector assemblies are used.

(b) The use of this direct fuel injector assembly causes the fuel to become highly atomized. At the same time, the fuel is mixed with a large amount of air and injected into the combustion chamber. As a result, fuel injection dissipation performance is improved to achieve a homogeneous air-fuel mixture, high performance and high engine output.

(c) Spray guide combustion which ignites stratified air-fuel mixture by discharging and draws in the flame enhances the TWC warm-up capability. As a result, emissions are reduced.

(d) A fuel injector seal is used for the cylinder internal pressure sealant and an insulator is used at the cylinder head sub-assembly contact area to reduce vibration and noise and improve sealing performance.

(e) An O-ring and backup ring* are used in the direct fuel injector assembly. As a result, the transmission of operation noise from the direct fuel injector assembly is reduced to improve quietness and ensure fastener airtightness.

NOTICE:

*: The backup ring is installed to securely support the rubber O-ring, which is subjected to high pressures. Take care to install the backup ring in the correct direction at the correct installation position.
