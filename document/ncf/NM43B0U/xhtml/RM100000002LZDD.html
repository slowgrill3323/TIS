OUTLINE

(a) Hybrid System (for HEV Models)

(1) The Parallel Hybrid makes it possible to directly transmit the engine motive force from the high-output T24A-FTS engine and the MG motive force from the MG in the hybrid module assembly, when combined with the automatic transmission, it demonstrates excellent acceleration capability.

(2) Hybrid electrical vehicles have 2 batteries that are used for different purposes. One is the HV supply battery assembly (nominal voltage of DC 288 V) that stores electrical power to drive the vehicle, and the other is the auxiliary battery (nominal voltage of DC 12 V) that supplies electrical power to the electrical components.

(3) Furthermore, hybrid electrical vehicles use a variable-voltage system consisting of a high-output HV supply battery assembly with a nominal voltage of DC 288 V and an inverter that converts direct current and alternating current

(4) Since hybrid electrical vehicles are not equipped with a conventional alternator, the high-voltage from the HV supply battery assembly is stepped down to approximately DC 14 V using a DC/DC converter in order to charge the auxiliary battery. Also, the HV supply battery assembly regularly charges and discharges within the constant State of Charge (SOC) range while the vehicle is running, so recharging from external power sources is not necessary.

(5) The engine can be started using the MG or the starter assembly, ensuring startability in cold regions. The engine can be started using the starter assembly at regular intervals to improve reliability.

(b) T24A-FTS Engine

(1) A T24A-FTS (2.4L) engine is used to achieve a higher dimension of balance between fuel efficiency, high performance, clean exhaust gas performance and quietness.

(2) A Direct injection 4-stroke gasoline engine Superior version (D-4S) system (which uses both a direct cylinder injection method and port injection method) is used to perform efficient combustion. In addition, a turbocharger, Electronic Throttle Control System-intelligent (ETCS-i) and Dual Variable Valve Timing-intelligent (Dual VVT-i) system (a continuously variable valve timing mechanism for intake and exhaust) are used to achieve low fuel efficiency, low emissions, high performance and high output.

(3) The D-4ST (Direct injection 4-stroke gasoline engine Superior version with Turbo) which performs comprehensive control over D-4S system and forced induction control of the turbocharger is used. With this, an increase in fuel economy and low speed torque has been realized due to the strong tumble flow created by the optimized combustion chamber shape and piston top shape along with forced induction high-level pressure control.

(4) The shape of the high-efficient intake port, offset crankshaft and combustion chamber is optimized to improve combustion efficiency, and the valve mechanism system is improved to reduce friction loss in each part of the engine. As a result, a high level of engine fuel efficiency is achieved.
