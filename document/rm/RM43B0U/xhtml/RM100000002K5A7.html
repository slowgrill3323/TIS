REMOVAL

CAUTION / NOTICE / HINT

The necessary procedures (adjustment, calibration, initialization, or registration) that must be performed after parts are removed, installed, or replaced during the transfer assembly removal/installation are shown below.

CAUTION:

  • To prevent burns, do not touch the engine, exhaust pipe or other high temperature components while the engine is hot.
  • Be sure to perform this procedure with several people as the transfer assembly is very heavy.

    *a

    Object Exceeding Weight Limit of Transmission Jack
  • Be sure to follow the procedure described in the repair manual, or the transmission jack may suddenly drop or a part may fall.

NOTICE:

Change the vehicle to H4F before removing the transfer assembly.

If the vehicle cannot be changed to H4F, since the new transfer assembly is set to H4F, connect all the connectors to the vehicle side wire harnesses to set the new transfer assembly to the drive state of the vehicle before installing the new transfer assembly.

13. REMOVE TRANSFER ASSEMBLY

(1) Using a transmission jack, plate lift attachments and a belt sling, etc., support the transfer assembly.

NOTICE:

Using a belt sling, etc., secure the transfer assembly to the transmission jack to prevent it from falling.

(2) Remove the 8 bolts and transfer assembly from the transmission assembly.

NOTICE:

  • Check the center of gravity while removing the transfer assembly.
  • Do not drop the transfer assembly.
  • Do not damage the lip of the transmission case oil seal.
