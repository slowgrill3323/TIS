Title: T24A-FTS (ENGINE CONTROL): SFI SYSTEM (for Gasoline Model): P10D31C; Charge Air Cooler Temperature Sensor 2 Bank 1 Circuit Voltage Out of Range; 2024 - 2026 MY Tacoma [12/2023 -        ]

DTC

P10D31C

Charge Air Cooler Temperature Sensor 2 Bank 1 Circuit Voltage Out of Range

DESCRIPTION

The engine has an intake air temperature sensor (No. 1 turbo pressure sensor), ECM internal temperature sensor and intake air temperature sensor (mass air flow meter sub-assembly).

The resistance of a thermistor within the intake air temperature sensor (No. 1 turbo pressure sensor), ECM internal temperature sensor and intake air temperature sensor (mass air flow meter sub-assembly) increases as the temperature decreases, and decreases as the temperature increases.

The voltage monitored by the ECM changes in accordance with the change in the resistance of the thermistor.

The ECM performs control based on these voltages.

HINT:

  • Waiting is required to prevent the temperature of the engine from affecting the readings. If the engine has been operated recently, it is not possible to accurately compare the readings.
  • For diagnosis, in order to duplicate the detection conditions of the DTC, it is necessary to park the vehicle for 7 hours. Parking the vehicle for 7 hours ensures that the actual temperature of the intake air temperature sensor (No. 1 turbo pressure sensor), ECM internal temperature and intake air temperature (for mass airflow meter sub-assembly) are very similar. When the vehicle has been parked for less than 7 hours, differences in the readings may exist, but this does not necessarily indicate a fault.

MONITOR DESCRIPTION

6, 6.5 and 7 hours after the engine has stopped, the ECM monitors the voltage of the intake air temperature sensor (No. 1 turbo pressure sensor), ECM internal temperature sensor and intake air temperature sensor (mass air flow meter sub-assembly).

The difference between the readings of the intake air temperature (No. 1 turbo pressure sensor) and intake air temperature (mass air flow meter sub-assembly) is more than 18.5°C (33.3°F), and the difference between the readings of the intake air temperature (No. 1 turbo pressure sensor) and ECM internal temperature is more than 16.5°C (29.7°F).

If the above conditions are met, the ECM monitors the voltage of the intake air temperature sensor (No. 1 turbo pressure sensor) and intake air temperature sensor (mass air flow meter sub-assembly) after turning the ignition switch to ON.

If the difference between the readings of the intake air temperature (No. 1 turbo pressure sensor) and intake air temperature (mass air flow meter sub-assembly) is more than 18.5°C (33.3°F), the ECM determines that there is a malfunction in the intake air temperature sensor (No. 1 turbo pressure sensor) circuit, and stores a DTC and illuminates the MIL.

CONFIRMATION DRIVING PATTERN

HINT:

  • After repair has been completed, clear the DTC and then check that the vehicle has returned to normal by performing the following All Readiness check procedure.

    Click here

  • When clearing the permanent DTCs, refer to the "CLEAR PERMANENT DTC" procedure.

    Click here

  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON [A].
  2. Clear the DTCs (even if no DTCs are stored, perform the clear DTC procedure).
  3. Turn the ignition switch off.
  4. With the engine stopped, leave the vehicle as is for 7.5 hours or more [B].
  5. Turn the ignition switch to ON.
  6. Wait 1 second [C].
  7. Enter the following menus: Powertrain / Engine / Trouble Codes [D].
  8. Read the pending DTCs.

    HINT:

    • If a pending DTC is output, the system is malfunctioning.
    • If a pending DTC is not output, perform the following procedure.
  9. Enter the following menus: Powertrain / Engine / Utility / All Readiness.
  10. Input the DTC: P10D31C.
  11. Check the DTC judgment result.

    HINT:

    • If the judgment result is NORMAL, the system is normal.
    • If the judgment result is ABNORMAL, the system is malfunctioning.
    • If the judgment result is INCOMPLETE, perform steps [B] through [D] again.
    • [B] to [D]: Normal judgment procedure.

      The normal judgment procedure is used to complete DTC judgment and also used when clearing permanent DTCs.

    • When clearing the permanent DTCs, do not disconnect the cable from the battery terminal or attempt to clear the DTCs during this procedure, as doing so will clear the universal trip and normal judgment histories.
