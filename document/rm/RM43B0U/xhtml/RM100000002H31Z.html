11. REMOVE DRIVE PLATE AND TORQUE CONVERTER SETTING BOLT

(1) Remove the 6 drive plate and torque converter setting bolts from the drive plate and ring gear sub-assembly.

HINT:

Turn the crankshaft to a position where the drive plate and torque converter setting bolts can be removed, and remove the drive plate and torque converter setting bolts while securing the crankshaft pulley bolt with a wrench.

12. REMOVE OIL COOLER HOSE HEAT INSULATOR

13. DISCONNECT OIL COOLER TUBE SUB-ASSEMBLY

*1

No. 1 Flexible Hose Clamp

*2

No. 2 Oil Cooler Tube Clamp

*3

No. 3 Oil Cooler Tube Clamp

-

-

(1) Slide the 2 hose clips and disconnect the 2 transmission oil hoses from the transmission oil thermostat.

HINT:

Use a container to catch any ATF which flows out of the 2 oil cooler tubes and automatic transmission assembly.

(2) Remove the bolt (A) and No. 1 flexible hose clamp from the No. 2 oil cooler tube clamp.

(3) Remove the bolt (B) and No. 1 flexible hose clamp from the No. 3 oil cooler tube clamp.

(4) Remove the bolt (C) and disconnect the oil cooler tube from the engine assembly.

HINT:

It is not necessary to remove the oil cooler tube clamps unless they are being replaced.

(5) Remove the bolt (D) and No. 3 oil cooler tube clamp from the engine assembly.

HINT:

It is not necessary to remove the oil cooler tube clamps unless they are being replaced.

15. REMOVE NO. 1 TRANSMISSION CONTROL CABLE BRACKET

16. SUPPORT AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION ASSEMBLY

(1) Support the automatic transmission assembly with a transmission jack.

NOTICE:

  • In order to protect the automatic transmission oil pan sub-assembly, place attachments on the transmission jack.
  • Make sure that the attachments and the automatic transmission oil pan sub-assembly are centered on the transmission jack.
  • To prevent the automatic transmission oil pan sub-assembly from deforming, do not place any attachments under the automatic transmission oil pan sub-assembly of the automatic transmission assembly.
  • Secure the automatic transmission assembly to the transmission jack using a belt, etc. to prevent it from falling.

19. DISCONNECT TRANSMISSION BREATHER HOSE SUB-ASSEMBLY

NOTICE:

  • The transmission breather hose sub-assembly can be disconnected without tilting the automatic transmission assembly.
  • When titling the automatic transmission assembly, do not tilt it excessively to allow the cooling fan and fan shroud to contact each other.

20. DISCONNECT WIRE HARNESS

NOTICE:

  • The wire harness can be disconnected without tilting the automatic transmission assembly.
  • When titling the automatic transmission assembly, do not tilt it excessively to allow the cooling fan and fan shroud to contact each other.

Pull Down the Lever

-

-

NOTICE:

Disconnect the claw, pull down the lever, and then disconnect the transmission wire connector.

21. REMOVE AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION ASSEMBLY

NOTICE:

  • The automatic transmission assembly can be removed without being tilted.
  • When titling the automatic transmission assembly, do not tilt it excessively to allow the cooling fan and fan shroud to contact each other.

(1) Remove the 7 bolts and automatic transmission assembly.

NOTICE:

  • Do not use excessive force to pry out the automatic transmission assembly when separating it from the engine assembly to prevent the knock pins from being damaged.
  • When removing the automatic transmission assembly, make sure that the oil cooler tube does not become damaged.
