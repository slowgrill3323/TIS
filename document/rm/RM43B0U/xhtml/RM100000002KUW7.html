Title: T24A-FTS (ENGINE CONTROL): SFI SYSTEM (for 1Motor-HEV Model): P007A11; Charge Air Cooler Temperature Sensor Bank 1 Circuit Short to Ground; 2024 - 2026 MY Tacoma HV [03/2024 -        ]

DTC

P007A11

Charge Air Cooler Temperature Sensor Bank 1 Circuit Short to Ground

DESCRIPTION

The intake air temperature sensor (for intercooler side) monitors the intake air temperature. The intake air temperature sensor has a built-in thermistor with a resistance that varies according to the temperature of the intake air. When the intake air temperature is low, the resistance of the thermistor increases. When the temperature is high, the resistance drops. These variations in resistance are transmitted to the ECM as voltage changes.

The intake air temperature sensor is powered by a 5 V supply from terminal THTA of the ECM, via resistor R.

Resistor R and the intake air temperature sensor are connected in series. When the resistance value of the intake air temperature sensor changes, according to changes in the intake air temperature, the voltage at terminal THTA also varies. Based on this signal, the ECM increases the fuel injection volume when the engine is cold to improve driveability.

HINT:

When DTC P007A11 is stored, the ECM enters fail-safe mode. During fail-safe mode, the intake air temperature is estimated to be 45°C (113°F) by the ECM. Fail-safe mode continues until a pass condition is detected.

MONITOR DESCRIPTION

The ECM monitors the sensor voltage and uses this value to calculate the intake air temperature. When the intake air temperature sensor (for intercooler side) output voltage deviates from the normal operating range, the ECM interprets this as a malfunction in the E.F.I. intake air temperature sensor circuit and stores this DTC.

Example:

If the intake air temperature sensor (for intercooler side) output voltage is less than 0.03 V for 3 seconds or more, the ECM store this DTC.

CONFIRMATION DRIVING PATTERN

  1. Clear the DTCs (even if no DTCs are stored, perform the clear DTC procedure).
  2. Turn the ignition switch off and wait for at least 30 seconds.
  3. Turn the ignition switch to ON.
  4. Wait 5 seconds or more.
  5. Enter the following menus: Powertrain / Engine / Trouble Codes.
  6. Read the pending DTCs.

    HINT:

    • If a pending DTC is output, the system is malfunctioning.
    • If a pending DTC is not output, perform the following procedure.
  7. Enter the following menus: Powertrain / Engine / Utility / All Readiness.
  8. Input the DTC: P007A11.
  9. Check the DTC judgment result.

    HINT:

    • If the judgment result shows NORMAL, the system is normal.
    • If the judgment result shows ABNORMAL, the system has a malfunction.
