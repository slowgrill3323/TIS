2. Wipe off any body protector adhesive residue with cleaner.

(2) Installation temperature

1. When the ambient temperature is below 15°C (59°F), perform the installation procedure after warming the vehicle body surface and body protector up to between 20 to 30°C (68 to 86°F) using a heat light. When the ambient temperature is above 35°C (95°F), cool the vehicle body surface and body protector down to between 20 to 30°C (68 to 86°F) prior to installation.

HINT:

  • The most appropriate temperature for installing the body protector is 25°C (77°F).
  • When the temperature is low, the body protector becomes stiff and comes off easily. When the temperature is high, the body protector elasticity increases.

(3) Before installation

1. Remove any coating roughness or dirt on and around the vehicle body surface where the body protector will be installed. If any roughness or dirt remains when pressing the body protector onto the surface, air will be trapped under the body protector and result in a poor appearance.

HINT:

Spray water on the shop floor to settle any dust.

(4) Key points for handling the body protector

1. The body protector bends and rolls up easily. Store the body protector between flat pieces of cardboard or other similar objects and keep it dry and flat.

NOTICE:

Do not bend the body protector or leave it in high temperature places.

(5) Key points for installation of the body protector (How to use a squeegee and installation procedure for flat surfaces)

To avoid air bubbles, slightly raise the part of the body protector that is going to be applied so that its adhesive surface does not touch the vehicle body prematurely. Tilt the squeegee at 40 to 50° (pressing forward) or 30 to 45° (pulling) to the vehicle body surface and press the body protector onto the vehicle body surface with a force of 20 to 30 N (2 to 3 kgf, 4.5 to 6.7 lbf) at a constant slow speed of 30 to 70 mm (1.19 to 2.75 in.) per second.

NOTICE:

  • Position the body protector accurately to achieve a neat finish and to avoid peeling.
  • The body protector cannot be reused because it deforms after removal.
  • Be sure to observe the specified pressing speed, force and angle of the squeegee to avoid wrinkles or air bubbles.

HINT:

  • Either angle of the squeegee (pressing forward or pulling) is acceptable.
  • Be sure to apply the body protector while removing the release paper 10 to 20 mm (0.394 to 0.787 in.) from the edge of the squeegee.

(6) Check after installation

After completing the installation, check if the body protector is installed neatly. If the body protector is not installed neatly, install a new body protector.

NOTICE:

Do not reuse the body protector.

(1) Clean the surface of the vehicle body.

1. Remove any remaining double-sided tape from the vehicle body.

2. Using a heat light, heat the vehicle body surface.

3. Wipe off any tape adhesive residue with cleaner.

NOTICE:

  • Installing the lower No. 1 side panel moulding LH with some double-sided tape remaining may cause poor adhesion. Perform this procedure until the tape is sufficiently removed.
  • Make sure to use a cloth when removing. Using a screwdriver, etc., may cause damage and poor adhesion.

(2) Using a heat light, heat the vehicle body and a new lower No. 1 side panel moulding LH.

(3) Attach the claw and double-sided tape and install the lower No. 1 side panel moulding LH as shown in the illustration.

HINT:

Apply pressure so that it does not lift up from the vehicle body.
