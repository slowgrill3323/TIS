PROCEDURE

1. CLEAN BACK WINDOW ASSEMBLY

*a

When reusing the back window assembly

-

-

(1) When reusing the back window assembly:

Using a scraper, grind off the adhesive and double-sided tape left on the back window assembly.

NOTICE:

Do not damage the back window assembly.

(2) Using non-residue solvent, clean the outer edge of the back window assembly.

NOTICE:

Be sure to clean the back window assembly even if it is new.

3. INSTALL BACK WINDOW GLASS SPACER (except Power Slide Type)

*a

Marking for Spacer Installation

-

-

(1) Install the back window glass spacer by the below procedure.

1. Apply primer to the installation part of the back window glass spacer.

HINT:

  • If primer is applied to an area that is not specified, wipe off the primer with a non-residue solvent before it dries.

2. Install 3 new back door glass spacer onto the back door glass, as shown in the illustration.

NOTICE:

  • Allow the primer to dry for 3 minutes or more.
  • Throw away any leftover primer.
  • Do not apply too much primer.

4. INSTALL BACK WINDOW ASSEMBLY

Adhesive

-

-

(1) Follow the procedure below to clean the installation surface of the vehicle body.

1. If the adhesive left on the installation surface of the vehicle body is significantly uneven, smooth it out using a utility knife, etc.

Standard Dimension:

Area

Dimension

a

1.0 to 3.0 mm (0.0394 to 0.118 in.)

NOTICE:

Make sure to leave some adhesive.

2. Using non-residue solvent, clean the installation surface of the vehicle body.

*a

Matchmarks

*b

Suction Cups

(1) Follow the procedure below to perform work before installing the back window assembly.

1. Using suction cups, position the glass so that the reference marks are aligned. Press it in gently along the rim.

2. Temporarily place the back window assembly on the body and position it so that the gap between the body and the edge of the back window assembly is the same width at the top and sides.

3. Attach adhesive tape on the back window assembly and body panel, and place matchmarks for installation using a pen (when using a new back window assembly).

NOTICE:

  • From inside the cabin, check that the back window glass stopper can be attached.
  • When reusing the back window assembly, check and correct the matchmark positions.

4. Remove the back window assembly.

5. Apply body primer to the exposed sections of the body surface on the vehicle side (where no adhesive remains).

NOTICE:

  • When applying the body primer, take care of the panel joints and spot welds.
  • Do not apply to the adhesive.
  • Do not apply too much body primer.

(1) Follow the procedure below to perform back window assembly installation.

1. Using the suction cups, align the matchmarks and install the back window glass.

NOTICE:

  • After the body side primer dries (in approximately 3 minutes), install the back window assembly.
  • From inside the cabin, check that the back window glass stopper can be attached.

2. Gently push the front surface of the back window assembly from outside of vehicle to fully contact with the adhesive.

HINT:

Pushing force: 98 N (10 kgf) or higher

3. Remove any excess adhesive or apply as necessary.

NOTICE:

Apply adhesive up to the edge of the back window assembly.

4. Remove any excess adhesive before it hardens.

5. Using protective tape, hold the back window assembly in place until the adhesive hardens.

NOTICE:

Follow the instructions for each adhesive manufacturer or the corresponding owner's manual for the minimum amount of time necessary for drying and prior to driving the vehicle after applying adhesive.

6. CHECK FOR LEAK AND REPAIR

(1) Follow the procedure below to check for water leak and repair.

1. Remove the protective tape.

2. After the adhesive has hardened, apply water from outside the vehicle and check for leaks from in the cabin.

3. If water leaks into the cabin, seal any leaks with adhesive after the water has dried.
