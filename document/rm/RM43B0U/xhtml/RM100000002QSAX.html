DTC

P071015

Transmission Fluid Temperature Sensor "A" Circuit Short to Battery or Open

DESCRIPTION

The No. 1 ATF temperature sensor installed in the transmission valve body assembly detects the ATF temperature inside the transmission oil pressure control circuit and inputs signals to the TCM according to the ATF temperature.

P071015

Transmission Fluid Temperature Sensor "A" Circuit Short to Battery or Open

Under any of the following conditions, the No. 1 ATF temperature sensor output value is less than -55°C (-67°F) for 1 second or more (1-trip detection logic):

  • 10 seconds or more have elapsed after the engine started when the engine coolant temperature and intake air temperature are higher than -30°C (-22°F).
  • 10 minutes or more have elapsed after the engine started when the engine coolant temperature and intake air temperature are -30°C (-22°F) or less.
  • Harness and connector

  • TCM

MONITOR DESCRIPTION

This DTC indicates an open or short in the No. 1 ATF temperature sensor circuit. The No. 1 ATF temperature sensor converts the ATF temperature into an electrical resistance value. Based on the resistance, the TCM determines the ATF temperature, and can detect an open or short in the No. 1 ATF temperature sensor circuit.

If the No. 1 ATF temperature sensor output value is less than -55°C (-67°F), the TCM interprets this as a malfunction of the No. 1 ATF temperature sensor or in the wire harness, illuminates the MIL and stores this DTC.
