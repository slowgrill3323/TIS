DTC

P085012

Park/Neutral Switch Circuit Short to Battery

DTC

P085014

Park/Neutral Switch Circuit Short to Ground or Open

DESCRIPTION

The engine stop and start ECU detects malfunctions by comparing the conditions of the shift position signal and NSW signal. If a malfunction is detected, the engine stop and start ECU blinks the stop and start cancel indicator, prohibits stop and start control and stores DTC P085012 or P085014.

P085012

Park/Neutral Switch Circuit Short to Battery

Both of the following conditions are met for 15 seconds or more (2 trip detection logic):

  • Ignition switch ON
  • NSW signal is off with the shift lever in P or N
  • Park/neutral position switch assembly
  • Engine stop and start ECU
  • ECM
  • TCM
  • Wire harness or connector

Park/Neutral Switch Circuit Short to Ground or Open

Both of the following conditions are met for 2 seconds or more (2 trip detection logic):

  • NSW signal is on with the shift lever in D or R

CAUTION / NOTICE / HINT

NOTICE:

  • Before replacing the engine stop and start ECU, read the number of starter operations, total number of engine starts and write it into a new engine stop and start ECU.

    Click here

  • After replacing the engine stop and start ECU or air conditioning amplifier assembly, reset and perform learning of the air conditioning information in the engine stop and start ECU.

    Click here

  • When the ECM is replaced on a vehicle with a non-specified battery, it is necessary to perform battery type switching.

    Click here

  • Perform Registration (VIN registration or frame number registration) after replacing the ECM.

    Click here

  • After replacing the engine stop and start ECU assembly, perform learning of the external backup boost converter (eco run vehicle converter assembly).

    Click here

  • Inspect the fuses for circuits related to this system before performing the following procedure.

HINT:

  • Using the GTS, read the freeze frame data before troubleshooting. System condition information is recorded as freeze frame data the moment a DTC is stored. This information can be useful when troubleshooting.

    Click here

  • DTCs for the stop and start system are not cleared even if the malfunction has been repaired. After repairing the malfunction, be sure to clear the DTCs.
