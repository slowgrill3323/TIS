Title: HYBRID / BATTERY CONTROL: HYBRID CONTROL SYSTEM (w/ 130A Generator): P300016; Hybrid/EV Battery Control System Circuit Voltage Below Threshold; 2025 - 2026 MY Tacoma HV [12/2024 -        ]

DTC

P300016

Hybrid/EV Battery Control System Circuit Voltage Below Threshold

DESCRIPTION

The hybrid vehicle control ECU alerts the driver and performs fail-safe control based on error signals received from the battery voltage sensor.

This DTC is stored if the HV battery is discharged excessively or the hybrid control system malfunctions.

HINT:

If the HV battery voltage has dropped due to a malfunction in other components such as the hybrid motor control inverter assembly or the hybrid module assembly, recharging the HV battery will be able to restore the voltage. If the HV battery voltage has dropped excessively after the vehicle was left for a long time, the HV battery will need to be replaced.

CONFIRMATION DRIVING PATTERN

HINT:

After repair has been completed, clear the DTC and then check that the vehicle has returned to normal by performing the following All Readiness check procedure.

Click here

  1. Connect the GTS to the DLC3.
  2. Turn the ignition switch to ON and turn the GTS on.
  3. Clear the DTCs (even if no DTCs are stored, perform the clear DTC procedure).
  4. Turn the ignition switch off and wait for 2 minutes or more.
  5. Turn the ignition switch to ON and turn the GTS on.
  6. Turn the ignition switch to ON (READY) with shift lever in P.
  7. Wait until the engine starts and then stops.
  8. Enter the following menus: Powertrain / Hybrid Control / Utility / All Readiness.
  9. Check the DTC judgment result.

    HINT:

    • If the judgment result shows NORMAL, the system is normal.
    • If the judgment result shows ABNORMAL, the system has a malfunction.
    • If the judgment result shows INCOMPLETE, perform driving pattern again.

HINT:

  • Thoroughly interview the customer as DTC P300016 may be stored if the SOC drops due to the vehicle being left with neutral (N) selected for a long time, running out of fuel, or the use of incorrect or low-quality fuel.
  • When the engine cannot be started, DTC P300016 may be stored because the HV battery cannot be charged.
