DTC

P279762

Electric/Auxiliary Transmission Fluid Pump "A" Performance/Stuck Off Signal Compare Failure

DESCRIPTION

Oil pump with motor assembly external factor, oil pump with motor assembly internal factor:

  • When the oil pump with motor assembly speed drops to less than the specified value and cannot be recovered, the oil pump with motor assembly does not operate, and the fuel economy worsens for reasons such as the inability to perform engine idling stop while waiting at traffic lights.

Transmission valve body assembly factor:

  • This DTC is output in situations where the oil pump with motor assembly is not used.

    When output, a failure of the check valve in the transmission valve body assembly is very likely instead of a failure of the oil pump with motor assembly.

MONITOR DESCRIPTION

Oil pump with motor assembly external factor:

  • When foreign matter is trapped temporarily in the pump gear, recovery is possible if the foreign matter is eliminated after several operations. However, if the foreign matter sticks to the pump gear and recovery is not possible, the TCM determines an oil pump motor abnormality, turns on the MIL and stores a DTC.

Oil pump with motor assembly internal factor:

  • When a failure occurs that decreases the torque of the internal motor (OPM) in the electric oil pump, since the OPM speed drops, the TCM determines an oil pump motor abnormality, turns on the MIL and stores a DTC.

Transmission valve body assembly factor:

  • When the check valve located in the transmission valve body assembly fails and excessive pressure is generated on the discharge port side inside the oil pump with motor assembly due to the oil pressure of the oil pump assembly, and the pump gear inside the oil pump with motor assembly rotates in reverse, the TCM determines a check valve abnormality, turns on the MIL and stores a DTC.
