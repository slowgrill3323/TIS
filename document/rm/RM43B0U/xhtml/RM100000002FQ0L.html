PROCEDURE

PROCEDURE

1. INSTALL NO. 1 STEERING RACK BOOT (for LH Side)

Grease

Grease Application Area

(1) As shown in the illustration, apply grease to the entire circumference rack boot contact surface of the rack end.

NOTICE:

Use the grease supplied with the No. 1 steering rack boot.

(2) As shown in the illustration, apply grease to the entire circumference of the rack housing.

NOTICE:

Use the grease supplied with the No. 1 steering rack boot.

(3) Install a new No. 1 steering rack boot to the groove of the rack housing.

NOTICE:

  • Be careful not to damage or twist the No. 1 steering rack boot.
  • Make sure that the No. 1 steering rack boot is free of rust and foreign matter.
  • Make sure that the boot is free of water, rust, foreign matter, etc.
  • Do not touch the inside of the No. 1 steering rack boot or rack bar when performing installation.

2. INSTALL NO. 1 STEERING RACK BOOT (for RH Side)

3. INSTALL STEERING RACK BOOT CLAMP (for LH Side)

Top of Vehicle

Rear of Vehicle

(1) Temporarily install a new steering rack boot clamp to the No. 1 steering rack boot.

NOTICE:

Do not damage the No. 1 steering rack boot.

(2) Make sure that the protrusion of the steering rack boot clamp is positioned within the area shown in the illustration.

(3) Using SST, install the steering rack boot clamp as shown in the illustration.

09521-24010

NOTICE:

  • Do not pinch the steering rack boot clamp excessively.
  • Be careful not to damage or twist the No. 1 steering rack boot.

(4) Remove SST and measure the clearance of the steering rack boot clamp.

Clearance:

6.0 mm (0.236 in.) or less

7. INSPECT RACK AND PINION POWER STEERING GEAR ASSEMBLY

(1) Using SST, rotate the pinion shaft to see if both the left and the right steering rack boots expand and contract smoothly.

(2) If the left and right steering rack boots do not expand and contract smoothly, use new steering rack boot clamps and reinstall the steering rack boots.
