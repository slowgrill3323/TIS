PROCEDURE

PROCEDURE

1. REMOVE NO. 2 AIR CLEANER INLET (except TRD Performance Intake or High Mount Air Intake)

2. REMOVE AIR CLEANER CAP SUB-ASSEMBLY (except TRD Performance Intake or High Mount Air Intake)

3. REMOVE AIR CLEANER FILTER ELEMENT SUB-ASSEMBLY (except TRD Performance Intake or High Mount Air Intake)

NOTICE:

Do not allow foreign matter to enter the air cleaner cap sub-assembly and air cleaner case sub-assembly.

4. REMOVE AIR CLEANER CASE LID (for TRD Performance Intake or High Mount Air Intake)

5. REMOVE AIR CLEANER FILTER ELEMENT SUB-ASSEMBLY (for TRD Performance Intake or High Mount Air Intake)

NOTICE:

Do not allow foreign matter to enter the air cleaner case sub-assembly.

6. INSPECT AIR CLEANER FILTER ELEMENT SUB-ASSEMBLY (except TRD Performance Intake or High Mount Air Intake)

HINT:

This procedure is only performed when the air cleaner filter element sub-assembly will be reused.

*a

Air Cleaner Filter Element Sub-assembly Intake Downstream Side (Air Cleaner Cap Sub-assembly Side)

-

-

(1) If the air cleaner filter element sub-assembly is dirty or has foreign matter adhered to it, clean the air cleaner filter element sub-assembly with compressed air.

NOTICE:

When cleaning the air cleaner filter element sub-assembly, blow the compressed air from the intake downstream side (from the air cleaner cap sub-assembly side).

(2) If the air cleaner filter element sub-assembly is still extremely dirty after cleaning it, replace the air cleaner filter element sub-assembly with a new one.

7. INSPECT AIR CLEANER FILTER ELEMENT SUB-ASSEMBLY (for TRD Performance Intake or High Mount Air Intake)

NOTICE:

Do not blow compressed air into the air cleaner filter element sub-assembly because the filter is a wet type air filter.

HINT:

For vehicles equipped with the TRD performance intake or High mount air intake, scan the QR code on the label on the back of the hood sub-assembly and visit the website to check the cleaning kit and how to clean the filter.
