When installing the radiator grille emblem, heat the radiator grille and radiator grille emblem using a heat light.

(1) Clean the surface of the radiator grille.

1. Using a heat light, heat the radiator grille surface.

2. Remove any remaining double-sided tape from the radiator grille.

3. Wipe off any tape adhesive residue with cleaner.

NOTICE:

  • Installing the radiator grille emblem with some double-sided tape remaining may cause poor adhesion. Perform this procedure until the tape is sufficiently removed.
  • Make sure to use a cloth when removing. Using a screwdriver, etc., may causedamage and poor adhesion.

(2) Using a heat light, heat the radiator grille and a new radiator grille emblem.

(3) Align the symbol emblem boss positions and the radiator grille positioning boss hole, and install the radiator grille emblem.

HINT:

Apply pressure so that it does not lift up from the radiator grille.

NOTICE:

Do not heat the vehicle body excessively.

2. Wipe off any stripe tape adhesive residue with cleaner.

(2) Installation temperature

1. When the ambient temperature is below 15°C (59°F), perform the installation procedure after warming the vehicle body surface and stripe tape up to between 20 to 30°C (68 to 86°F) using a heat light. When the ambient temperature is above 35°C (95°F), cool the vehicle body surface and stripe tape down to between 20 to 30°C (68 to 86°F) prior to installation.

HINT:

  • The most appropriate temperature for installing the stripe tape is 25°C (77°F).
  • When the temperature is low, the stripe tape becomes stiff and comes off easily. When the temperature is high, the stripe tape elasticity increases.

(3) Before installation

1. Remove any coating roughness or dirt on and around the vehicle body surface where the stripe tape will be installed. If any roughness or dirt remains when pressing the stripe tape onto the surface, air will be trapped under the stripe tape and result in a poor appearance.

HINT:

Spray water on the shop floor to settle any dust.

(4) Key points for handling the stripe tape

1. The stripe tape bends and rolls up easily. Store the stripe tape between flat pieces of cardboard or other similar objects and keep it dry and flat.

NOTICE:

Do not bend the stripe tape or leave it in high temperature places.

(5) Key points for installation of the stripe tape (How to use a squeegee and installation procedure for flat surfaces)

To avoid air bubbles, slightly raise the part of the stripe tape that is going to be applied so that its adhesive surface does not touch the vehicle body prematurely. Tilt the squeegee at 40 to 50° (pressing forward) or 30 to 45° (pulling) to the vehicle body surface and press the stripe tape onto the vehicle body surface with a force of 20 to 30 N (2 to 3 kgf, 4.5 to 6.7 lbf) at a constant slow speed of 30 to 70 mm (1.19 to 2.75 in.) per second.

NOTICE:

  • Position the stripe tape accurately to achieve a neat finish and to avoid peeling.
  • The stripe tape cannot be reused because it deforms after removal.
  • Be sure to observe the specified pressing speed, force and angle of the squeegee to avoid wrinkles or air bubbles.

HINT:

  • Either angle of the squeegee (pressing forward or pulling) is acceptable.
  • Be sure to apply the stripe tape while removing the release paper 10 to 20 mm (0.394 to 0.787 in.) from the edge of the squeegee.

(6) Check after installation

After completing the installation, check if the stripe tape is installed neatly. If the stripe tape is not installed neatly, install a new stripe tape.

NOTICE:

Do not reuse the stripe tape.
