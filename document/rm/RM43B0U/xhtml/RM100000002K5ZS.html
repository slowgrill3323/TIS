If the runout is more than the maximum, replace the differential ring gear with a new one.

4. INSPECT DIFFERENTIAL RING GEAR BACKLASH

(1) Using a dial indicator, measure the backlash of the differential ring gear.

Standard backlash:

0.10 to 0.20 mm (0.00394 to 0.00787 in.)

If the backlash is not within the specification, adjust the side bearing preload or perform repairs as necessary.

HINT:

Perform the measurement at 3 or more positions around the circumference of the differential ring gear.

5. INSPECT TOTAL PRELOAD

(1) Using a torque wrench, measure the preload with the teeth of the differential drive pinion and differential ring gear in contact.

If the result is not as specified, adjust the preload.

(1) Place matchmarks on the differential bearing cap and differential carrier.

(2) Remove the 4 bolts and 2 differential bearing caps.

(3) Remove the 2 plate washers.

HINT:

  • Put identification marks on the plate washers to show the location for installation (LH side (back side) or RH side (teeth side)), or keep them separate so that they can be distinguished.
  • Measure the thickness of each plate washers and note it.

11. REMOVE DIFFERENTIAL CASE ASSEMBLY

(1) Remove the differential case assembly from the differential carrier.

HINT:

Tag the 2 rear differential case bearings (outer) so that they can be reinstalled in the correct locations.

12. REMOVE DIFFERENTIAL DRIVE PINION

(1) Using a press, remove the differential drive pinion with rear differential drive pinion bearing spacer from the differential carrier.

NOTICE:

Do not drop the differential drive pinion.

HINT:

The rear drive pinion front bearing (inner) remains on the differential carrier side.

14. REMOVE REAR DRIVE PINION REAR BEARING (INNER)

(1) Using SST and a press, press out the rear drive pinion rear tapered roller bearing (inner) from the differential drive pinion.

09555-55010

NOTICE:

Do not drop the differential drive pinion.

HINT:

If the differential drive pinion or differential ring gear is damaged, replace them as a set.

17. REMOVE REAR DRIVE PINION REAR BEARING (OUTER)

(1) Using a brass bar and hammer, tap out the rear drive pinion rear bearing (outer) and adjusting shim from the differential carrier.

HINT:

If the bearing is damaged during removal, replace it.
