Trailer Camera Image is not Displayed or Blinking Intermittently

DESCRIPTION

The display signal from the wireless camera is transmitted to the display.

WIRING DIAGRAM

CAUTION / NOTICE / HINT

HINT:

  • Perform pairing after replacing or removing and installing the wireless camera.

    Click here

  • Inspect the fuse for circuits related to this system before performing the following procedure.
  • Inner rear view mirror may flicker if wireless connection is poor. Due to inherit wireless limitation (out of performance range or scope) .

PROCEDURE

PROCEDURE

1.

CHECK TRAILER CAMERA SYSTEM (CURRENT SIGNAL STRENGTH)

(a) Ensure the trailer camera system is tested without obstacles blocking line of sight or strong nearby Wi-Fi interference.

(b) Navigate to AUX menu screen on the multi-display and enter the diagnostic menu.

(c) Locate wireless signal strength menu parameter and take note of current signal strength when camera is 914.5 cm (30.00 ft.) behind the rear of the vehicle.

(d) Check the flicker condition is present when the wireless camera is 30 feet behind rear of vehicle.

OK:

Flicker does not occurs.

OK

END (TRAILER CAMERA SYSTEM IS WORKING WITHIN THE DESIGNED PERFORMANCE SCOPE)
NG

GO TO STEP 2

2.

CHECK TRAILER CAMERA SYSTEM (CURRENT SIGNAL STRENGTH)

(a) Bring the wireless camera closer to vehicle rear until flickering stops or signal strength improves.

(b) Move back away from vehicle rear until flicker or signal strength is equal to the original recorded value.

(c) Check the distance from the telephone antenna assembly is 914.5 cm (30.00 ft.) or more.

OK:

The distance from the telephone antenna is 914.5 cm (30.00 ft.) or more.

OK

END (TRAILER CAMERA SYSTEM IS WORKING WITHIN THE DESIGNED PERFORMANCE SCOPE)
