CAUTION / NOTICE / HINT

NOTICE:

  • While the auxiliary battery is connected, even if the ignition switch is off, the brake control system activates when the brake pedal is depressed or any door courtesy switch turns on. Therefore, when servicing the brake system components, do not operate the brake pedal or open/close the doors while the battery is connected.
  • After the ignition switch is turned off, there may be a waiting time before disconnecting the negative (-) auxiliary battery terminal.

PROCEDURE

PROCEDURE

1. PERFORM ACCUMULATOR PRESSURE ZERO DOWN

Click here

2. DRAIN BRAKE FLUID

NOTICE:

If brake fluid leaks onto any painted surface, immediately wash it off.

3. REMOVE LOWER NO. 1 INSTRUMENT PANEL AIRBAG ASSEMBLY

Click here

4. REMOVE PUSH ROD PIN

Click here

5. SEPARATE ENGINE ROOM MAIN WIRE

NOTICE:

Be careful not to allow any brake fluid to enter the connector.

Release the lock lever

Disconnect the connector

(1) Disconnect the brake master cylinder with pump assembly connector.

(2) Disconnect the brake fluid level warning switch connector and disengage the clamp.

(3) Release the lock lever and disconnect the connector from the brake booster with master cylinder assembly.

6. REMOVE NO. 5 BRAKE TUBE CLAMP

NOTICE:

Do not kink or damage the brake tubes.

7. REMOVE BRAKE TUBE

(1) Using a union nut wrench, remove the 2 brake tubes.

NOTICE:

  • Do not kink or damage the brake tubes.
  • Do not allow any foreign matter such as dirt or dust to enter the brake tubes from the connecting parts.

8. REMOVE BRAKE MASTER CYLINDER WITH PUMP ASSEMBLY

(1) Remove the 4 nuts and brake master cylinder with pump assembly.

NOTICE:

  • Do not apply excessive force to the brake tubes.
  • Be careful not to allow any brake fluid to enter the connector.
  • Do not carry the brake master cylinder with pump assembly by the parts shown in the illustration.

    *a

    No. 1 Skid Control ECU

    *b

    No. 1 Reservoir Hose and No. 2 Reservoir Hose

    *c

    Master Cylinder Push Rod Clevis

    *d

    Boot

    *e

    Brake Master Cylinder Reservoir

    *f

    No. 1 Brake Actuator Tube

    *g

    Accumulator

    *h

    Brake Booster Pump

9. REMOVE BRAKE MASTER CYLINDER GASKET

10. REMOVE BRAKE BOOSTER BRACKET

(1) w/ Booster Bracket Stopper Ring:

Remove the 2 booster bracket stopper rings.

HINT:

The 2 booster bracket stopper rings are used as a jig during assembly at the factory. When installing the brake booster with master cylinder assembly, it is not necessary to install the 2 booster bracket stopper rings.

(2) Remove the brake booster bracket.

11. REMOVE BRAKE MASTER CYLINDER GASKET

12. REMOVE NO. 1 RESERVOIR HOSE

13. REMOVE NO. 2 RESERVOIR HOSE

14. REMOVE NO. 1 BRAKE ACTUATOR TUBE

(1) Using a union nut wrench, remove the No. 1 brake actuator tube.

NOTICE:

  • Do not kink or damage the brake line.
  • Do not allow any foreign matter such as dirt or dust to enter the brake line from the connecting parts.

15. REMOVE BRAKE BOOSTER WITH ACCUMULATOR PUMP ASSEMBLY

Click here

16. REMOVE BRAKE MASTER CYLINDER BRACKET

(1) Using a 5 mm long hexagon socket wrench, remove the bolt and brake master cylinder bracket.

17. REMOVE NO. 2 WIRE CLAMP BRACKET

(1) Using a 5 mm long hexagon socket wrench, remove the bolt and No. 2 wire clamp bracket.

18. REMOVE NO. 1 BRAKE POWER SUPPLY BRACKET

(1) Using a 5 mm long hexagon socket wrench, remove the 2 bolts and No. 1 brake power supply bracket.

19. REMOVE NO. 2 BRAKE POWER SUPPLY BRACKET

(1) Using a 5 mm long hexagon socket wrench, remove the 2 bolts and No. 2 brake power supply bracket.

20. REMOVE MASTER CYLINDER PUSH ROD CLEVIS

HINT:

Perform this procedure only when replacement of the master cylinder push rod clevis is necessary.
