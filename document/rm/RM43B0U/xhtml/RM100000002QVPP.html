ON-VEHICLE INSPECTION

CAUTION / NOTICE / HINT

CAUTION:

  • To prevent injury due to contact with an operating V-ribbed belt or cooling fan, keep your hands and clothing away from the V-ribbed belt and cooling fans when working in the engine compartment with the engine running or the ignition switch ON.
  • To prevent burns, do not touch the engine, exhaust manifold or other high temperature components while the engine is hot.

HINT:

After charging the auxiliary battery, perform the integrated current value initialization.

PROCEDURE

PROCEDURE

1. CHECK AUXILIARY BATTERY

(a) Check that the auxiliary battery cables are connected to the correct terminals.

HINT:

If they are not, connect them properly.

(b) Check the auxiliary battery for damage and deformation. If severe damage, deformation or leakage is found, replace the auxiliary battery.

(c) Check the electrolyte level in each cell.

(1) For maintenance-free batteries:

  • If the electrolyte level is below the lower line, replace the auxiliary battery.

(2) For non-maintenance-free batteries:

  • If the electrolyte level is below the lower line, add distilled water to each cell. Then, recharge the auxiliary battery and check the electrolyte specific gravity.

    Standard Specific Gravity:

    1.25 or higher at 20°C (68°F)

2. CHECK AUXILIARY BATTERY VOLTAGE

(a) Turn the ignition switch off and turn on the headlights for 30 seconds. This will remove the surface charge from the auxiliary battery.

6. INSPECT GENERATOR ASSEMBLY WIRING

(a) Visually check the generator assembly wiring.

(1) Check that the wiring is in good condition.

7. CHECK FOR ABNORMAL NOISE

(a) Check for abnormal noises from the generator assembly.

(1) When the generator assembly is generating power.

  1. Remove the IGCT NO.2 fuse from the No. 1 engine room relay block and junction block assembly.
  2. Check that no abnormal noises are heard from the generator assembly while the engine is running.

    If noise occurs, check the generator assembly.

  3. Install the IGCT NO.2 fuse.

    NOTICE:

    If the ignition switch is turned to ON (READY) with the fuse removed, other DTCs will be stored. Be sure to clear the DTCs after the inspection.

(2) When the generator assembly is not generating power.

  1. Check that no abnormal noises are heard from the generator assembly while the engine is running.

    If noise occurs, check the generator assembly.

8. INSPECT CHARGING CIRCUIT WITHOUT LOAD

CAUTION:

  • To prevent injury due to contact with an operating V-ribbed belt or cooling fan, keep your hands and clothing away from the V-ribbed belt and cooling fans when working in the engine compartment with the engine running or the ignition switch ON.
  • To prevent burns, do not touch the engine, exhaust manifold or other high temperature components while the engine is hot.

Pre-procedure1

(a) Connect a voltmeter and an ammeter to the charging circuit as follows.

HINT:

If a auxiliary battery/generator assembly tester is available, connect the tester to the charging circuit in accordance with the manufacturer's instructions.

(1) Disconnect the wire from terminal B of the generator assembly and connect it to the negative (-) lead of the ammeter.

(2) Connect the ammeter positive (+) lead to terminal B of the generator assembly.

(3) Connect the voltmeter positive (+) lead to terminal B of the generator assembly.

(4) Ground the voltmeter negative (-) lead.

(5) Connect the GTS to the DLC3.

(6) Turn the ignition switch to ON.

9. INSPECT CHARGING CIRCUIT WITH LOAD

CAUTION:

  • To prevent injury due to contact with an operating V-ribbed belt or cooling fan, keep your hands and clothing away from the V-ribbed belt and cooling fans when working in the engine compartment with the engine running or the ignition switch ON.
  • To prevent burns, do not touch the engine, exhaust manifold or other high temperature components while the engine is hot.

Pre-procedure1

(a) Connect the GTS to the DLC3.

(b) Turn the ignition switch to ON.

(c) Put the engine in Inspection Mode (for measuring Exhaust Gas).

Powertrain > Hybrid Control > Utility

Tester Display

Inspection Mode

(d) Set the instruction voltage to 14.8 V.

Powertrain > Engine > Active Test

Tester Display

Control the Voltage of Alternator

(e) Start the engine.

(f) Wait 60 seconds or more until the voltage stabilises.

(g) With the engine running at 2000 rpm, turn the high beam headlights on and turn the heater blower switch to the "HI" position.

Procedure1

(h) Check the reading on the ammeter.

Standard Current:

The current value increases to a value that is 10 A or more than the result of the unloaded test.

If the result is not as specified, repair or replace the generator assembly.

HINT:

If the auxiliary battery is fully charged, the reading will sometimes be less than the standard current. In this case, add more electrical load (operate the wipers, rear window defogger, etc.) and check the reading on the ammeter again.

Post-procedure1

(i) None

10. CHECK AMD TERMINAL

CAUTION:

  • Orange wire harnesses and connectors indicate high-voltage circuits. To prevent electric shock, always follow the procedure described in the repair manual.

    Click here

  • To prevent electric shock, wear insulated gloves when working on wire harnesses and components of the high voltage system.

(a) Remove the service plug grip.

(b) Check that the AMD terminal is connected securely, and there is no contact problem.

HINT:

If there are any arc marks, replace the affected parts.

*1

AMD Terminal (Hybrid Motor Control Inverter Assembly Side)

*2

AMD Terminal (No. 1 Engine Room Relay Block and No. 1 Junction Block Assembly Side)

(c) Check that the nut for the AMD terminal is tightened to the specified torque.

10 N·m {102 kgf·cm, 89 in·lbf}

No. 1 Engine Room Relay Block and No. 1 Junction Block Assembly Side :

10 N·m {102 kgf·cm, 89 in·lbf}

HINT:

If there are no arc marks and the AMD terminal connection is faulty, connect the AMD terminal securely.

(d) Install the service plug grip.

11. CHECK DC/DC CONVERTER FUNCTION

(a) Be sure to disconnect the cable from the negative (-) auxiliary battery terminal before disconnecting the terminal B from the generator assembly.

NOTICE:

  • After the ignition switch is turned off, there may be a waiting time before disconnecting the auxiliary negative (-) battery terminal.

    Click here

  • When disconnecting and reconnecting the auxiliary battery, there is an automatic learning function that completes learning when the respective system is used.

(b) After disconnecting the terminal B, wrap it with vinyl insulating tape.

NOTICE:

  • A short circuit to ground may occur if the terminal B is disconnected before the cable is disconnected from the negative (-) auxiliary battery terminal. If a short circuit to ground occurs, a fusible link or fuse may break.
  • Secure the disconnected B terminal to prevent it from coming into contact with other objects and causing a short circuit.
