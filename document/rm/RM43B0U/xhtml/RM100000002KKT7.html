(1) Pinch the tabs of the retainer to detach the lock claws and pull it up.

NOTICE:

  • Remove any dirt or foreign matter on the tube connector before performing this work.
  • Do not allow any scratches or foreign matter to get on the parts when disconnecting them as the tube connector has an O-ring that seals the pipe (charcoal canister sub-assembly).
  • Perform this work by hand. Do not use any tools.
  • Do not forcibly bend, twist or turn the fuel tank vent hose.
  • Protect the disconnected parts by covering them with plastic bags after disconnecting the fuel tank vent hose sub-assembly.
  • If the tube connector and pipe (charcoal canister sub-assembly) are stuck, push and pull to release them.

7. REMOVE CANISTER PUMP MODULE (LEAK DETECTION PUMP SUB-ASSEMBLY)

HINT:

Perform this procedure only when replacement of the canister pump module (leak detection pump sub-assembly) is necessary.

(1) Before removing the canister pump module (leak detection pump sub-assembly), clean the charcoal canister sub-assembly by blowing air into it to ensure that the charcoal canister sub-assembly is free of foreign matter.

NOTICE:

  • Make sure to clean the charcoal canister sub-assembly using air only.
  • Do not use gasoline, thinners or solvents.

(2) Detach the 2 claws and remove the canister pump module (leak detection pump sub-assembly) from the filter assembly.

HINT:

Tape the screwdriver tip before use.

(3) Check if the filter assembly contains foreign matter such as mud or water.

  1. Visually check that the inside of the filter assembly is free of foreign matter.
  2. Hold the filter assembly upside down to make sure that it is free of foreign matter.

    If the filter assembly contains foreign matter, replace the canister pump module (leak detection pump sub-assembly).
