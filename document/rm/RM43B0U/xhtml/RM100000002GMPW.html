Title: T24A-FTS (ENGINE CONTROL): SFI SYSTEM (for Gasoline Model): P011A62,P111A62; Engine Coolant Temperature Sensor 1/2 Signal Compare Failure; 2024 - 2026 MY Tacoma [12/2023 -        ]

DTC

P011A62

Engine Coolant Temperature Sensor 1/2 Signal Compare Failure

DTC

P111A62

Engine Coolant Temperature Sensor 1/3 Signal Compare Failure

DESCRIPTION

In vehicles equipped with a water control valve, the coolant temperature is detected in 3 locations (inlet, cylinder head and outlet) to provide appropriate coolant temperature control for operating valves.

During complete water stop in the warm-up process, the outlet engine coolant temperature sensor that was conventionally used for temperature adjustment does not perform temperature detection because there is no coolant flow. Therefore, the cylinder head engine coolant temperature sensor is used to monitor and control the temperature during water stop instead of the conventional outlet engine coolant temperature sensor. In conditions other than complete water stop in the warm-up process, the outlet engine coolant temperature sensor is used to control the coolant temperature the same as in conventional temperature adjustment.

The inlet engine coolant temperature sensor is used to improve responsiveness after warm-up.

All of the following conditions are met (2 trip detection logic):

  1. The battery voltage is 10 V or higher.
  2. 5 hours or more have elapsed since the engine stopped on the previous trip.

MONITOR DESCRIPTION

P011A62:

After the engine has stopped for 5 hours or more, the ECM monitors the voltage of the engine coolant temperature sensor (for outlet side) and engine coolant temperature sensor (for cylinder head side). If the difference between the readings of the engine coolant temperature (for outlet side) and engine coolant temperature sensor (for cylinder head side) is higher than 20°C (36°F), the ECM determines that there is a malfunction in the engine coolant temperature sensor (for outlet side) circuit or engine coolant temperature sensor (for cylinder head side) circuit, and stores a DTC and illuminates the MIL.

P111A62:

After the engine has stopped for 5 hours or more, the ECM monitors the voltage of the engine coolant temperature sensor (for outlet side) and engine coolant temperature sensor (for water inlet housing side). If the difference between the readings of the engine coolant temperature (for outlet side) and engine coolant temperature sensor (for water inlet housing side) is higher than 20°C (36°F), the ECM determines that there is a malfunction in the engine coolant temperature sensor (for outlet side) circuit or engine coolant temperature sensor (for water inlet housing side) circuit, and stores a DTC and illuminates the MIL.

  8. Input the DTC: P011A62 or P111A62.

    HINT:

      The normal judgment procedure is used to complete DTC judgment and also used when clearing permanent DTCs.
