Title: AL80E (AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION / TRANSAXLE): AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION SYSTEM: P074571; Pressure Control Solenoid "A" Performance/Stuck Off Actuator Stuck; 2024 - 2026 MY Tacoma [12/2023 -        ]

DTC

P074571

Pressure Control Solenoid "A" Performance/Stuck Off Actuator Stuck

DESCRIPTION

The TCM detects the gear position based on signals from the transmission revolution sensor (NT) and transmission revolution sensor (SP2).

If the detected gear position differs from the commanded gear position, the TCM detects a mechanical problem in the related solenoid valve, transmission valve body assembly or automatic transmission assembly.

Pressure Control Solenoid "A" Performance/Stuck Off Actuator Stuck

One of the following conditions is met (2-trip detection logic):

  • When the TCM commands 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th or 5th gear, the transmission revolution sensor is detected 5 times in neutral for 0.5 seconds or more
  • When the TCM commands 6th gear, the actual gear is detected 5 times as 4th gear or 5th gear for 0.5 seconds or more*
  • When the TCM commands 7th gear, the actual gear is detected 5 times as 3rd gear or 5th gear for 0.5 seconds or more*
  • When the TCM commands 8th gear, the actual gear is detected 5 times as 2nd gear or 5th gear for 0.5 seconds or more*
  • When the TCM commands to switch from 5th gear to 6th gear, it is detected 5 times that the gear does not switch to 6th gear for 0.8 seconds or more
  • Solenoid (SL1) valve
  • Solenoid (SLT) valve
  • Transmission valve body assembly
  • Automatic transmission assembly (clutch, brake, gear, etc.)

MONITOR DESCRIPTION

The TCM commands gear shifts by turning the solenoid valves on and off. According to the input shaft revolution (speed) and output shaft revolution (speed), the TCM detects the actual gear (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th, 6th, 7th, 8th or reverse gear position). When the gear commanded by the TCM and the actual gear are not the same, the TCM illuminates the MIL and stores a DTC.
