HINT:

  • Use the same procedure for the RH and LH sides.
  • The procedure listed below is for the LH side.

PROCEDURE

PROCEDURE

1. REMOVE CYLINDER BOOT

(1) Using a screwdriver with its tip wrapped with protective tape, remove the 4 set rings from the disc brake cylinder assembly.

(2) Using a screwdriver with its tip wrapped with protective tape, remove the 4 cylinder boots from the disc brake cylinder assembly.

NOTICE:

Be careful not to damage the front disc brake piston and disc brake cylinder assembly.

2. REMOVE FRONT DISC BRAKE PISTON

*a

Wooden Block

-

-

(1) Install a wooden block between the pistons of the disc brake cylinder assembly to stop them coming out.

(2) Using an air gun, gradually apply compressed air until the 4 front disc brake pistons touch the wooden block.

CAUTION:

  • Do not insert your hand between the pistons to hold the disc brake cylinder assembly.
  • When compressed air is blown in, the front disc brake piston may jump out quickly and trap your fingers.

NOTICE:

Do not allow any brake fluid to spatter.

(3) Remove the Wooden block.

(4) Remove the 4 front disc brake pistons from the disc brake cylinder assembly.

NOTICE:

Be careful not to damage the disc brake cylinder assembly and front disk brake pistons.

3. REMOVE PISTON SEAL

(1) Using a screwdriver with its tip wrapped with protective tape, remove the 4 piston seals from the disc brake cylinder assembly.

NOTICE:

Do not damage the inner surface or piston seal groove of the disc brake cylinder assembly.
