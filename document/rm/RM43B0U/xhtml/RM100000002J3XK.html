UNDER HOOD

GENERAL NOTES

  • Maintenance requirements vary depending on the country.
  • Check the maintenance schedule in the owner's manual supplement.
  • Following the maintenance schedule is mandatory.
  • Determine the appropriate time to service the vehicle using either distance driven or time elapsed, whichever reaches the specification first.
  • Maintain similar intervals between periodic maintenance unless noted.
  • Failing to check each vehicle part could lead to poor engine performance and increased exhaust emissions.

WINDSHIELD WASHER FLUID

(a) Check that there is sufficient fluid in the tank.

ENGINE COOLANT LEVEL

(a) Check that the coolant level is between the "FULL" and "LOW" lines on the see-through reservoir.

RADIATOR AND HOSES

(a) Check that the front of the radiator is clean and free of leaves, dirt and bugs.

(b) Check the hoses for cracks, kinks, rotting or loose connections.

BRAKE FLUID LEVEL

(a) Check that the brake fluid levels are near the upper level line on the see-through reservoirs.

ENGINE DRIVE BELT

(a) Check the drive belt for fraying, cracks, wear or oiliness.

ENGINE OIL LEVEL

(a) Check that the engine oil level is between the full level and low level marks on the dipstick with the engine turned off.

AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION FLUID LEVEL

(a) Visually check the overflow plug and oil pan for fluid leaks or traces of fluid.

MANUAL TRANSAXLE OIL

(a) Visually check the overflow plug and oil pan for fluid leaks or traces of fluid.

EXHAUST SYSTEM

(a) Check for unusual exhaust sounds or abnormal exhaust fumes. If a problem is found, locate the cause and correct it.
