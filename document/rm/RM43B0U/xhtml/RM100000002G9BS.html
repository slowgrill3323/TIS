PROCEDURE

1. INSTALL NO. 3 REAR SPOILER SEAL

NOTICE:

When installing the No. 3 rear spoiler seal, heat the No. 3 rear spoiler seal and rear spoiler using a heat light.

Standard Value:

Part

Temperature

Rear Spoiler

20 to 30°C (68 to 86°F)

No. 3 rear spoiler seal

20 to 30°C (68 to 86°F)

CAUTION:

  • Do not touch the heat light and heated parts.
  • Touching the heat light may result in burns.
  • Touching heated parts for a long time may result in burns.

*a

Heated Part

*b

Heat Light

NOTICE:

Do not heat the No. 3 rear spoiler seal and rear spoiler excessively.

*a

Mark-off Line

-

-

(1) Clean the surface of the rear spoiler.

1. When reusing the rear spoiler, remove remaining double-sided tape from the rear spoiler.

2. Using a heat light, heat the rear spoiler surface.

3. Wipe off any tape adhesive residue with cleaner.

NOTICE:

  • Installing the No. 3 rear spoiler seal with some double-sided tape remaining may cause poor adhesion. Perform this procedure until the tape is sufficiently removed.
  • Make sure to use a cloth when removing. Using a screwdriver, etc., may cause damage and poor adhesion.

(2) Primer application.

1. Apply primer to the rear spoiler on the installation area of the No. 3 rear spoiler seal using a brush or felt.

NOTICE:

  • Replace the brush or felt if it is dirty or has become hardened.
  • Keep any painted surface free from primer.
  • If the primer contacts a painted surface, it may leave light primer marks. Therefore, use protective tape when using liquid primer.
  • Do not touch surfaces to which primer has been applied until the No. 3 rear spoiler seal has been attached.

2. Let the primer dry sufficiently.

NOTICE:

Do not touch applied surfaces until the primer is dry.

Recommended drying time:

10 minutes or more (at 23°C (73°F))

(3) Using a heat light, heat the rear spoiler and a new No. 3 rear spoiler seal.

(4) Install the No. 3 rear spoiler seal so that it fits inside the mark-off line as shown in the illustration.

NOTICE:

The application strength of the No. 3 rear spoiler seal will weaken if reapplied. If reapplication is necessary, be sure to replace it with a new one.

HINT:

Apply pressure so that it does not lift up from the rear spoiler.
