Title: BRAKE (REAR): REAR BRAKE: INSTALLATION; 2024 MY Tacoma Tacoma HV [12/2023 - 03/2024]

INSTALLATION

CAUTION / NOTICE / HINT

NOTICE:

  • Immediately after installing the brake pads, the braking performance may be reduced. Always perform a road test in a safe place while paying attention to the surroundings.
  • After replacing the rear disc brake pads, the brake pedal may feel soft due to clearance between the rear disc brake pads and rear disc. Depress the brake pedal several times until the brake pedal feels firm.
  • After replacing the rear disc brake pads, always perform a road test to check the braking performance and check for vibrations.

HINT:

  • Use the same procedure for the RH side and LH side.
  • The following procedure is for the LH side.

PROCEDURE

PROCEDURE

1. INSTALL REAR DISC

*a

Matchmark

-

-

(1) Align the matchmarks of the rear disc and rear axle hub and bearing assembly, and install the rear disc.

NOTICE:

When replacing the rear disc with a new one, select the installation position where the rear disc has minimal runout.

2. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE CYLINDER MOUNTING

Torque:

205 N·m {2090 kgf·cm, 151 ft·lbf}

3. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE BUSHING DUST BOOT

Lithium Soap Base Glycol Grease

-

-

(1) Apply a light layer of lithium soap base glycol grease to the entire circumference of 2 new rear disc brake bushing dust boots.

HINT:

Apply more than 0.3 g (0.01 oz) of lithium soap base glycol grease to each rear disc brake bushing dust boot.

(2) Install the 2 rear disc brake bushing dust boots to the rear disc brake cylinder mounting.

4. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE CYLINDER SLIDE BUSHING

Lithium Soap Base Glycol Grease

-

-

(1) Apply a light layer of lithium soap base glycol grease to the contact surface of the rear disc brake rear cylinder slide pin.

(2) Install a new rear disc brake cylinder slide bushing to the rear disc brake rear cylinder slide pin.

5. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE CYLINDER SLIDE PIN

*1

Rear Disc Brake Cylinder Slide Pin

*2

Rear Disc Brake Rear Cylinder Slide Pin

Lithium Soap Base Glycol Grease

-

-

(1) Apply a light layer of lithium soap base glycol grease to the sliding part and the sealing surfaces of the rear disc brake cylinder slide pin and rear disc brake rear cylinder slide pin.

(2) Install the rear disc brake cylinder slide pin and rear disc brake rear cylinder slide pin to the rear disc brake cylinder mounting.

(3) Push the rear disc brake cylinder slide pin and rear disc brake rear cylinder slide pin into each rear disc brake bushing dust boot to engage the pins to the boots.

6. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE PAD SUPPORT PLATE

NOTICE:

Be sure to install each rear disc brake pad support plate in the correct position and direction.

7. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE ANTI-SQUEAL SHIM KIT

Click here

8. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE PAD KIT

NOTICE:

Install the rear disc brake pad so that the rear disc brake pad wear indicator plate is mounted on the lower side of the vehicle.

9. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE CYLINDER ASSEMBLY

(1) Hold the 2 rear disc brake cylinder slide pins, and install the rear disc brake cylinder assembly to the rear disc brake cylinder mounting with 2 bolts.

Torque:

55 N·m {561 kgf·cm, 41 ft·lbf}

NOTICE:

If bleeding of air from the rear brake cylinder assembly will be performed, reuse the bolts. If bleeding of air from the rear brake cylinder assembly will not be performed, install new bolts.

10. INSTALL PARKING BRAKE ACTUATOR SEAL

NOTICE:

If bleeding of air from the rear brake cylinder assembly will be performed, reuse the parking brake actuator seal. If bleeding of air from the rear brake cylinder assembly will not be performed, install a new parking brake actuator seal.

13. CONNECT REAR FLEXIBLE HOSE

(1) Connect the rear flexible hose to the rear disc brake cylinder assembly with a new union bolt and a new gasket.

30 N·m {306 kgf·cm, 22 ft·lbf}

NOTICE:

Install the rear flexible hose lock securely into the lock hole in the rear disc brake cylinder assembly.

15. BLEED REAR DISC BRAKE CYLINDER ASSEMBLY

CAUTION:

If the rear disc brake cylinder assembly has been disassembled, perform air bleeding for the rear disc brake cylinder assembly.

NOTICE:

  • Perform air bleeding while maintaining the brake fluid level between the MAX and MIN lines on the brake fluid reservoir.
  • Do not allow brake fluid to contact any painted surface. If brake fluid leaks onto any painted surface, immediately wash it off.

HINT:

  • Use the same procedure for the RH side and LH side.
  • The following procedure is for the LH side.
  • While performing air bleeding of the rear disc brake cylinder assembly, the bolts can be reused during the bleeding procedure. After air bleeding is complete, replace the bolts with new ones.

(1) Perform the following procedure:

  1. Perform the procedure to enter rear disc brake pad replacement mode 5 times.

    Click here

  2. Remove the brake master cylinder reservoir filler cap assembly.
  3. Force air within the rear disc brake cylinder assembly to accumulate at the rear disc brake bleeder plug.(*1)
    1. Disconnect the No. 2 parking brake wire assembly connector.

      Click here

    2. Hold the 2 rear disc brake cylinder slide pins and remove the 2 bolts and separate the rear disc brake cylinder assembly from the rear disc brake cylinder mounting.
    3. Hold the rear disc brake cylinder assembly horizontally, then tilt it 45° toward the bleeder plug side as shown in the illustration.
    4. Temporarily install the rear disc brake cylinder assembly to the rear disc brake cylinder mounting with the 2 bolts.
    5. Add brake fluid to keep the level between the MIN and MAX lines of the reservoir while bleeding the brakes.

      NOTICE:

      • Make sure that there is sufficient brake fluid in the reservoir.
      • Do not remove the filter from the brake master cylinder reservoir assembly and be sure to fill the brake master cylinder reservoir assembly with new brake fluid to avoid any potential contamination of the brake system. Contamination, for example by dirt particles or mineral oil, could lead to functional brake problems.
      • If brake fluid leaks onto any painted surface, immediately wash it off.
    6. Remove the rear disc brake bleeder plug cap.
    7. Connect a vinyl tube to the rear disc brake bleeder plug.
  4. Depress the brake pedal several times, and then loosen the rear disc brake bleeder plug with the pedal depressed.(*2)
  5. When fluid stops coming out, tighten the rear disc brake bleeder plug and release the brake pedal.(*3)

    NOTICE:

    Repeat steps (*2) through (*3) until there are no signs of air in the brake fluid.

  6. Repeat steps (*1) through (*3) 3 times.

    NOTICE:

    If there is still air in the system after performing steps (*1) through (*3) 3 times, repeat the steps (*1) through (*3) until the air has been bled.

  7. Tighten the rear disc brake bleeder plug completely.

    Torque:

    8.3 N*m (85 kgf*cm, 73 in.*lbf)

  8. Install the rear disc brake bleeder plug cap to the rear disc brake bleeder plug.
  9. Hold the 2 rear disc brake cylinder slide pins, and install the rear disc brake cylinder assembly to the rear disc brake cylinder mounting with 2 new bolts.

    Torque:

    55 N*m (561 kgf*cm, 41 ft.*lbf)

  10. Connect the No. 2 parking brake wire assembly connector.

    Click here

  11. Inspect the brake fluid level in the reservoir.

    Click here

  12. Install the brake master cylinder reservoir filler cap assembly.
  13. Turn the ignition switch off.
  14. Clear the DTCs.

    Click here
